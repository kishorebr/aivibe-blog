<p>OpenAI CEO Sam Altman's house may have been the target of a second attack after San Francisco Police Department arrested two suspects for a reported shooting in the Russian Hill neighborhood. The SFPD said in a <a target="_blank" class="no-affiliate-link link" href="https://www.sanfranciscopolice.org/news/sfpd-arrests-suspects-involved-shooting-26-044" data-i13n="elm:context_link;elmt:doNotAffiliate;slk:press release;cpos:1;pos:1">press release</a> that police officers responded to a "suspicious occurrence of possible shots fired" at around 5:56 AM ET / 2:56 AM PT on Sunday, April 12.</p><p>SFPD's Special Investigation Division took over the case and have since detained both 25-year-old Amanda Tom and 23-year-old Muhamad Tarik Hussein, seizing three firearms in the process with the help of a warrant. The two suspects were charged with negligent discharge.</p><p>According to the initial police report, as reported by <a target="_blank" class="no-affiliate-link link" href="https://sfstandard.com/2026/04/12/sam-altman-s-home-targeted-second-attack/" data-i13n="elm:context_link;elmt:doNotAffiliate;slk:The San Francisco Standard;cpos:2;pos:1"><em>The San Francisco Standard</em></a>, two people inside a Honda sedan stopped in front of Altman's property that spans from Chestnut Street to Lombard Street. The police report also noted that the passenger appeared to fire a round at the Lombard Street side of Altman's property. The property's security personnel reported hearing a gunshot and there was surveillance footage that recorded the incident, according to the report.</p><p>This could be the second instance of violence targeting Altman and his residence in a matter of days. On Friday, a 20-year-old man allegedly hurled a <a target="_blank" class="no-affiliate-link link" href="https://www.engadget.com/ai/a-man-allegedly-threw-a-molotov-cocktail-at-sam-altmans-house-210444731.html" data-i13n="elm:context_link;elmt:doNotAffiliate;slk:Molotov cocktail;cpos:3;pos:1">Molotov cocktail</a> at Altman's home, which caused a fire on one of the property's exterior gates, according to SFPD. <em>The San Francisco Standard</em> reported that there were no injuries in either incident. Daniel Moreno-Gama, the 20-year-old suspect from Texas, was charged on April 13 with murder and attempted arson.
