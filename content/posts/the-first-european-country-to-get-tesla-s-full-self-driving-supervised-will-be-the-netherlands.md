<p>Tesla's Full Self-Driving (Supervised) is ready to make its European debut, and it's starting with the Netherlands. According to <a data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1" href="https://x.com/teslaeurope/status/2042709396111724639?s=20">Tesla Europe</a>, the automaker's driver assistance system was approved in the Netherlands and will start rolling out shortly. <a data-i13n="elm:context_link;elmt:doNotAffiliate;cpos:2;pos:1" class="no-affiliate-link" href="https://www.rdw.nl/en/news/2026/rdw-explanation-of-european-type-approval-tesla-with-provisional-validity-in-the-netherlands">RDW</a>, the country's regulatory authority on vehicles, confirmed the news with a post on its website about Tesla receiving a type approval for its Full Self-Driving (Supervised) system.</p>
<p>According to the RDW, Tesla's <a data-i13n="elm:context_link;elmt:doNotAffiliate;cpos:3;pos:1" class="no-affiliate-link" href="https://www.engadget.com/tesla-halves-the-price-of-its-full-self-driving-supervised-subscription-to-99-per-month-215321467.html">Full Self-Driving (Supervised)</a> "has been extensively examined and tested for more than one and a half years on our test track and on public roads," and concluded that it was a "positive contribution" to road safety. However, RDW pointed out that a Tesla with FSD Supervised was not "self-driving," adding that the "driver remains responsible and must always remain in control."</p>
<span id="end-legacy-contents"></span><p>With Dutch approvals, Tesla notched its first regulatory green light for FSD use in Europe. The RDW also added that Tesla's FSD Supervised could get "possible later admittance in all member states of the European Union" thanks to its approvals. Tesla has been working on bringing its automated driving features to other regions, including Europe and China, as detailed in a <a data-i13n="elm:context_link;elmt:doNotAffiliate;cpos:4;pos:1" class="no-affiliate-link" href="https://www.engadget.com/transportation/evs/tesla-says-full-self-driving-will-be-ready-for-europe-and-china-in-early-2025-033012374.html">roadmap</a> posted in 2024. In the meantime, the automaker's software has been mired in several safety investigations from the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration. The <a data-i13n="elm:context_link;elmt:doNotAffiliate;cpos:5;pos:1" class="no-affiliate-link" href="https://www.nhtsa.gov/?nhtsaId=EA26002">latest development</a> comes from a probe that targets collisions when using FSD, including the supervised version, in reduced road visibility conditions.</p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/transportation/the-first-european-country-to-get-teslas-full-self-driving-supervised-will-be-the-netherlands-170157644.html?src=rss
