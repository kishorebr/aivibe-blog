<p>Amazon is reportedly adding Teresa Palmer (<em>The Fall Guy, Warm Bodies, Hacksaw Ridge</em>) to its pantheon of Norse gods for its <em>God of War</em> TV show adaptation. As first reported by <a data-i13n="elm:context_link;elmt:doNotAffiliate;cpos:1;pos:1" class="no-affiliate-link" href="https://deadline.com/2026/01/teresa-palmer-cast-prime-video-god-of-war-1236684477/"><em>Deadline</em></a>, Palmer will play Sif, Thor&#39;s wife and eventual leader of the Aesir, in the live-action adaptation. It may not carry as much weight as the casting of the video game&#39;s protagonist that was revealed earlier this week to be <a data-i13n="elm:context_link;elmt:doNotAffiliate;cpos:2;pos:1" class="no-affiliate-link" href="https://www.engadget.com/entertainment/tv-movies/ryan-hurst-cast-as-kratos-for-live-action-god-of-war-show-211655396.html">Ryan Hurst</a>, but it could hint at the direction the TV show may take.</p>

<p>While Sif plays a minor role in the <a data-i13n="elm:context_link;elmt:doNotAffiliate;cpos:3;pos:1" class="no-affiliate-link" href="https://www.engadget.com/god-of-war-ragnarok-review-ps5-162534113.html"><em>God of War Ragnarok</em></a> game, the early casting confirmation could indicate that the showrunner, Ronald D. Moore, may give the character a more involved role. In <em>God of War Ragnarok</em>, Sif is known as Odin&#39;s diplomat before the events of Ragnarok, where she becomes the new leader of the Aesir, one of two tribes of Norse gods. Notably, Amazon&#39;s adaptation is still missing the casting confirmations for Atreus, Thor, Odin and many other Norse gods seen in the video game. Even so, the <em>God of War</em> TV show has already secured at least <a data-i13n="elm:context_link;elmt:doNotAffiliate;cpos:4;pos:1" class="no-affiliate-link" href="https://www.engadget.com/entertainment/tv-movies/amazons-god-of-war-adaptation-gets-a-two-season-order-133059803.html">two seasons</a>.</p>
