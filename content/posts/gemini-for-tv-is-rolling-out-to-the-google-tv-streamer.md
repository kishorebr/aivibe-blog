<p>Google is continuing to push its Gemini AI assistant into as many of its products as possible. The <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://blog.google/products/google-nest/google-tv-streamer-gemini-tv-now-available/" data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1">latest</a> to get the treatment is Google TV Streamer, where Gemini for TV will begin rolling out today and over the coming weeks. The Google TV Streamer had a few minor AI features when we <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/entertainment/streaming/google-tv-streamer-review-a-great-side-piece-for-your-tv-with-a-dash-of-smart-home-chops-and-inessential-ai-160034550.html" data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1">reviewed</a> the hardware, but this new voice-controlled application of Google&#39;s Gemini is more robust. It can offer suggestions for viewing based on the preferences of whoever is watching or provide additional context for the content playing.&nbsp;</p><p>We knew from <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/mobile/smartphones/everything-google-announced-at-the-android-show-174155773.html" data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1">The Android Show</a> this spring that Google was planning to bring Gemini to additional platforms, including televisions. A lot of those plans have been coming to fruition in the final quarter of 2025; for instance, last month Google opened early access to <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/ai/early-access-for-gemini-home-voice-assistant-is-now-available-heres-how-to-get-it-191250927.html" data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1">Gemini for Home</a>, which brings a specially-designed AI assistant to its Google Home smart speaker hardware.</p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/ai/gemini-for-tv-is-rolling-out-to-the-google-tv-streamer-220448361.html?src=rss
