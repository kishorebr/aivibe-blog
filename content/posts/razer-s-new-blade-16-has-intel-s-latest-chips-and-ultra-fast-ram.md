<p>After leaning into <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/gaming/pc/razer-put-a-waifu-in-a-bottle-at-ces-2026-205315908.html" data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1">some questionable AI antics</a> at CES 2026, Razer is making some altogether more <a target="_blank" class="no-affiliate-link link" href="https://www.razer.com/newsroom/product-news/2026-blade-16/" data-i13n="elm:context_link;elmt:doNotAffiliate;cpos:2;pos:1">practical updates</a> to its 16-inch laptop by giving it newer chips and faster RAM. The new Razer Blade 16 features Intel's new Core Ultra chips and speedy LPDDR5X-9600 MHz RAM, and is available to order today for $3,500.</p><p>The Razer Blade 16 is designed to split the difference between the portable Razer Blade 14 and the monstrous Razer Blade 18, mostly by being thin but offering improved performance. Razer says the 2026 Blade 16 is 0.59 inches (14.9mm) at its thinnest point, which matches the thinness of <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/computing/laptops/razers-new-blade-16-laptop-is-its-thinnest-model-yet-140032671.html" data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1">the 2025 Blade 16</a>. The laptop also has a similar 16-inch QHD+, 240Hz OLED screen to last year's model, though the company says it's 100 nits brighter than before. Port selection also remains respectable: the laptop includes three USB 3.2 Gen 2 Type-A ports, a Thunderbolt 4 port, a Thunderbolt 5 port, a full-sized HDMI 2.1 port and a UHS-II SD card reader.</p><figure><img src="https://d29szjachogqwa.cloudfront.net/images/user-uploaded/razer-blade-16-with-monitor.jpg" data-crop-orig-src="https://d29szjachogqwa.cloudfront.net/images/user-uploaded/razer-blade-16-with-monitor.jpg" style="height:4320px;width:7680px;" alt="A Razer Blade 16 laptop viewed from the front next to a monitor." data-uuid="ce45b44a-c393-43fb-b150-f14cbe4db548"><figcaption></figcaption><div class="photo-credit">Razer</div></figure><p>The real notable upgrade is Razer's switch from AMD Ryzen chips to new Intel Core Ultra 9 386H chips on the 2026 Razer Blade 16. <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/computing/ces-2026-intel-hopes-its-core-ultra-series-3-chips-are-the-start-of-a-comeback-000155611.html" data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1">The new Core Ultra chips</a> are some of Intel's first processors made with its new <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/computing/intels-next-gen-chip-manufacturing-process-reportedly-hits-snag-after-failing-crucial-tests-172220843.html" data-i13n="cpos:5;pos:1">18A process</a> and pitched as the company's comeback. Razer says the new chip es 16 cores and an integrated NPU that "provides up to 50 TOPS" for things like image generation and live translation, which the Razer Blade 16 supports natively as <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/microsoft-unveils-copilot-pcs-with-generative-ai-capabilities-baked-in-170445370.html" data-i13n="cpos:6;pos:1">a Copilot+PC</a>. The efficiency of the new chip also contributes to the laptop’s up to 15 hours of battery life. Of course, if you want power, the Razer Blade 16 has it: the laptop includes NVIDIA's GeForce RTX 50 Series Laptop GPUs and up to 64Gb of LPDDR5X-9600MHz RAM, which should give the Razer Blade 16 plenty of pep for games. </p><p>Hardware upgrades don't come cheap, and the higher $3,500 starting price of the Razer Blade 16 — which includes 32GB of RAM, 1TB of storage and a GeForce RTX 5080 GPU at a minimum — is likely reflective of <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/gaming/pc/hp-says-ram-now-accounts-for-more-than-a-third-of-its-pc-costs-192914150.html" data-i13n="cpos:7;pos:1">the growing cost</a> of memory and storage that's already <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/ai/samsung-says-ram-costs-will-likely-lead-to-price-hikes-soon-170653524.html" data-i13n="cpos:8;pos:1">negatively&nbsp;impacting</a> the PC industry.</p><p>The Razer Blade 16 is available to purchase now through Razer’s website.</p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/computing/laptops/razers-new-blade-16-has-intels-latest-chips-and-ultra-fast-ram-185858799.html?src=rss
