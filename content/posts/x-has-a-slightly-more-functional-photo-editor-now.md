<p>X is <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://x.com/nikitabier/status/2041594683072413883?s=20" data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1">rolling out an update</a> to its in-app photo editor that gives users the ability to edit photos with xAI&#39;s Grok, blur faces and overlay text on images. The new editing features, in particular the addition of text-based edits via an AI assistant, bring it much closer in capabilities to dedicated photo apps like Google Photos. </p><p>As part of the update, users are able to prompt Grok to make edits to a photo just by typing out what they want to see. The example video shared by Nikita Bier, X&#39;s Head of Product, showed an image being edited so that it appeared to be hanging in a museum, but simpler tweaks are presumably possible, too. The feature is similar to the &quot;<a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/ai/google-photos-conversational-editing-is-rolling-out-to-android-users-170057906.html" data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1">conversational editing</a>&quot; Google added to Google Photos in September 2025, where users can prompt Gemini to adjust the background of an image or make other edits. X&#39;s new editor also includes tools for blurring or redacting parts of an image, drawing on images and overlaying text.</p><div><blockquote class="twitter-tweet" style="width:640px;height:941px;"><p lang="en" dir="ltr">Ladies and gentlemen, we&#39;re launching a brand new Photo Editor in our post composer.<br><br>It has long-overdue features like drawing &amp; text. But we also included special add-ons that are unique to X:<br><br>• Edit with words, powered by Grok <br>• Add a blur to redact parts of the photo… <a href="https://t.co/38Zaw8b5jl">pic.twitter.com/38Zaw8b5jl</a></p>— Nikita Bier (@nikitabier) <a href="https://twitter.com/nikitabier/status/2041594683072413883?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw">April 7, 2026</a></blockquote>


</div><p>X used to take a far more freewheeling approach to editing photos with Grok, by allowing any user to reply to a post tagging the AI assistant and requesting an edit. After users <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/ai/grok-generated-an-estimated-3-million-sexualized-images--including-23000-of-children--over-11-days-175053250.html" data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1">reportedly generated millions of sexualized images</a> using the feature, including some of children, <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/ai/x-says-grok-will-no-longer-edit-images-of-real-people-into-bikinis-231430257.html" data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1">X limited</a> Grok&#39;s image generating abilities to paying subscribers and removed the AI&#39;s ability to create images of real people in bikinis, underwear and other suggestive clothing. </p><p>xAI, the parent company of X, is currently the defendant in <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/social-media/xai-is-being-sued-by-teens-who-say-grok-created-csam-using-their-photos-200102733.html" data-i13n="cpos:5;pos:1">a class action lawsuit</a> from three teenagers who allege their photos were used to create child exploitation material with Grok. X is also <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/big-tech/eu-launches-second-investigation-into-groks-nonconsensual-image-generation-113239967.html" data-i13n="cpos:6;pos:1">being investigated</a> in the European Union over similar reports that its platform was used to create nonconsensual sexual images.</p><p>X’s updated photo editor is available on iOS now and coming to Android “soon,” according to the company.</p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/apps/x-has-a-slightly-more-functional-photo-editor-now-210825833.html?src=rss
