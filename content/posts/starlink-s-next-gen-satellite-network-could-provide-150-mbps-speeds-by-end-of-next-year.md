<p><a data-i13n="elm:context_link;elmt:doNotAffiliate;cpos:1;pos:1" class="no-affiliate-link" href="https://www.engadget.com/mobile/smartphones/t-mobiles-starlink-satellite-service-now-works-with-a-handful-of-apps-165732659.html">Starlink</a> is getting ready to launch its second generation of satellites, and it&#39;s expected to match the speeds of a traditional terrestrial network. During a <a data-i13n="elm:context_link;elmt:doNotAffiliate;cpos:2;pos:1" class="no-affiliate-link" href="https://www.mwcbarcelona.com/agenda/sessions/6087-keynote-2-transforming-tomorrows-connected-world#watch">keynote</a> at Mobile World Congress, Starlink execs detailed the roadmap for the company&#39;s upgrade towards the next generation of satellites called V2.</p>
<p>&quot;The goal of Starlink Mobile ... is to provide a terrestrial-like connectivity when you&#39;re connected to the satellite system,&quot; Michael Nicolls, SpaceX&#39;s senior vice president of Starlink engineering, said during the MWC keynote. &quot;In the right conditions, it should look and feel like you&#39;re connected to a high-performing 5G terrestrial network.&quot;</p>
<span id="end-legacy-contents"></span><p>Nicolls detailed that the V2 satellite constellation could offer download speeds up to 150 Mbps in ideal conditions, comparing it to a broadband experience. According to <a data-i13n="elm:context_link;elmt:doNotAffiliate;cpos:3;pos:1" class="no-affiliate-link" href="https://x.com/Starlink/status/2028284498555924671?s=20">Starlink</a>, next-gen satellites will offer 100 times the data density of its predecessors, which should help users with faster streaming and browsing as well as more reliable voice calls. Notably, Nicolls added that the V2 satellite constellation would offer better coverage to Earth&#39;s polar regions, which are known to have unreliable coverage with traditional networks.</p>
<p>Nicolls said that SpaceX is planning to send out more than 50 V2 satellites on each SpaceX launch starting in mid-2027, with a goal of building out a full constellation in six months. Outside its MWC presser, Starlink also announced a partnership with German telecommunications company <a data-i13n="elm:context_link;elmt:doNotAffiliate;cpos:4;pos:1" class="no-affiliate-link" href="https://www.telekom.com/en/media/media-information/archive/telekom-and-starlink-satellite-to-mobile-for-europe-1103000">Deutsche Telekom</a>.
