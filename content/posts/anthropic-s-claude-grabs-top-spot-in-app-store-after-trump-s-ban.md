<p>Anthropic may have lost out on doing business with the <a data-i13n="elm:context_link;elmt:doNotAffiliate;cpos:1;pos:1" class="no-affiliate-link" href="https://www.engadget.com/ai/trump-orders-federal-agencies-to-drop-anthropic-services-amid-pentagon-feud-222029306.html">US government</a>, but it&#39;s gained enough popularity to earn the number one spot on the App Store&#39;s <a data-i13n="elm:context_link;elmt:doNotAffiliate;cpos:2;pos:1" class="no-affiliate-link" href="https://apps.apple.com/us/iphone/charts/36?chart=top-free">Top Free Apps</a> leaderboard. At the top, Claude beat out both ChatGPT and Google Gemini, which respectively sit at the second and third spots on Apple&#39;s free apps charts.</p>
<p>The sudden surge in user downloads isn&#39;t random. It follows news that <a data-i13n="elm:context_link;elmt:doNotAffiliate;cpos:3;pos:1" class="no-affiliate-link" href="https://truthsocial.com/@realDonaldTrump/posts/116144552969293195">President Trump</a> has barred any federal agency from using Anthropic&#39;s Claude or other AI tools after the AI company refused to concede on certain guardrails. After <a data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1" href="https://www.engadget.com/ai/anthropic-refuses-to-bow-to-pentagon-despite-hegseths-threats-085553126.html">declining</a> to have its AI models be used for mass domestic surveillance and fully autonomous weapons, Anthropic was also threatened with a &quot;supply-chain risk&quot; label by the Department of Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth. The very public spat led to a wave of user support that finally allowed Claude to dethrone OpenAI&#39;s ChatGPT on the App Store as the most downloaded free app.</p>
<span id="end-legacy-contents"></span><p>While OpenAI has stepped into Anthropic&#39;s shoes after agreeing to a deal with the Department of Defense, the CEO still offered up some thoughts about the debacle during an AMA on X. Even though Claude is a competing model, <a data-i13n="elm:context_link;elmt:doNotAffiliate;cpos:5;pos:1" class="no-affiliate-link" href="https://x.com/sama/status/2027917750858092921?s=20">Sam Altman</a> said that Anthropic&#39;s supply-chain risk designation was &quot;a very bad decision&quot; that he&#39;s hoping gets reversed. On top of that, OpenAI&#39;s CEO called Anthropic&#39;s blacklisting &quot;an extremely scary precedent,&quot; but he&#39;s &quot;still hopeful for a much better resolution.&quot;</p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/big-tech/anthropics-claude-grabs-top-spot-in-app-store-after-trumps-ban-193610130.html?src=rss
