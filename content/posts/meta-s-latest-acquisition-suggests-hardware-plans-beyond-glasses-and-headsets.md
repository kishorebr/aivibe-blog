<p>Meta has acquired Limitless, the maker of an AI-powered &quot;Pendant,&quot; to work on building consumer hardware for the company, the startup announced via a <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uuGTJzl1OVU" data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1">YouTube video</a> and <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.limitless.ai/" data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1">blog post</a>. So far, Meta has focused on selling <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/ar-vr/meta-quest-3s-review-impressive-vr-for-300-130013596.html" data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1">VR headsets</a> and <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/wearables/ray-ban-meta-2nd-gen-review-smart-glasses-are-finally-getting-useful-124720393.html" data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1">AI smart glasses</a>. Now the company seems interested in branching out.</p><p>&quot;Meta recently announced a new vision to bring personal superintelligence to everyone and a key part of that vision is building incredible AI-enabled wearables. We share this vision and we&#39;ll be joining Meta to help bring our shared vision to life,&quot; Limitless CEO Dan Siroker said in the post announcing the acquisition.</p><p>Limitless&#39; first product was <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://techcrunch.com/2022/11/01/rewind-wants-to-revamp-how-you-remember-with-millions-from-a16z/" data-i13n="cpos:5;pos:1">Rewind</a>, desktop productivity software that recorded everything you did on your computer and turned it into a searchable database you interacted with via a chatbot. The company later <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://techcrunch.com/2024/04/17/a16z-backed-rewind-pivots-to-build-ai-powered-pendant-to-record-your-conversations/" data-i13n="cpos:6;pos:1">expanded into hardware</a> with Pendant, essentially a clip-on Bluetooth microphone that applies the same concept (privacy concerns be damned) to the things you say or hear throughout the day.</p><p>The company plans to support its existing Pendant customers &quot;for at least another year,&quot; but will no longer sell the wearable going forward. Current customers will be able to access all the features of Pendant without having to pay for a subscription, though Limitless says availability will vary per region.
