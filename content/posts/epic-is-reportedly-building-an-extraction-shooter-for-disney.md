<p>Besides a wealth of <em>Fortnite </em>skins based on Disney IP, it hasn&#39;t really been clear what the entertainment company has gotten in return for<a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/disney-is-investing-15-billion-in-epic-games-to-create-a-games-and-entertainment-universe-215015443.html" data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1"> its $1.5 billion investment in Epic</a> from 2024. That could change this November, <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-10/epic-games-pins-fortnite-comeback-on-disney-partnership?accessToken=eyJhbGciOiJIUzI1NiIsInR5cCI6IkpXVCJ9.eyJzb3VyY2UiOiJTdWJzY3JpYmVyR2lmdGVkQXJ0aWNsZSIsImlhdCI6MTc3NTg0NjcwOSwiZXhwIjoxNzc2NDUxNTA5LCJhcnRpY2xlSWQiOiJUREFKUDFLSVVQU1cwMCIsImJjb25uZWN0SWQiOiJGRUIzODlCNUI2ODI0RTY0QjY5MENEODE1RTBDREZGRCJ9.h6cBDcEh8Tu5LgBFeDz9XXDvKXrwqtOWNoYlxwJvx18&amp;leadSource=uverify%20wall" data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1"><em>Bloomberg</em> reports</a>, when Epic releases a Disney-themed extraction shooter. The game is one of three Disney projects the publisher is currently working on, and is reportedly expected to be Epic&#39;s comeback after the company <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/gaming/epic-is-laying-off-more-than-1000-workers-citing-a-downturn-in-fortnite-engagement-154436905.html" data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1">laid off 1,000 employees in March</a> due to a &quot;downturn in <em>Fortnite</em> engagement.&quot; </p><p>The game is reportedly similar to <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/epic-games-shows-off-more-of-fortnites-rocket-racing-mode-ahead-of-its-launch-023944541.html" data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1"><em>Arc Raiders</em></a>, a multiplayer shooter where players fight for resources before escaping through an extraction point, but with Disney characters fighting enemies instead of post-apocalyptic survivors. <em>Bloomberg</em> writes that internal reviewers have worried that the game&#39;s mechanics are &quot;not very original,&quot; but the project is the most promising of the three Epic is developing. The second title received middling internal reviews, according to <em>Bloomberg</em>, and Epic moved resources off the third project &quot;after reports that Disney was disappointed by Epic’s release timeline.&quot;</p><p>“This is not reflective of the ambitions of the Disney collaboration,” Liz Markman, Senior Director of Communications at Epic Games, said in a statement. “We are building a new games and entertainment universe of Disney experiences.”</p><p>While details of Epic&#39;s work for Disney are coming into focus, it&#39;s still unclear whether this new extraction shooter will be a standalone game or incorporated as a mode in <em>Fortnite</em>. In its efforts to sell the title as a &quot;multiverse&quot; and a competitor to Roblox, Epic has introduced <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/fortnite-festival-tries-to-bring-back-the-heyday-of-music-gaming-153624729.html" data-i13n="cpos:5;pos:1">multiple</a> <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/epic-games-shows-off-more-of-fortnites-rocket-racing-mode-ahead-of-its-launch-023944541.html" data-i13n="cpos:6;pos:1">games</a> inside <em>Fortnite</em> over the last few years with distinct mechanics. The developer announced that it would shut down three of those titles — <em>Rocket Racing</em>, <em>Ballistic</em> and <em>Fortnite Festival Battle Stage</em> — as part of its recent round of layoffs. According to current and former Epic employees <em>Bloomberg</em> spoke to, several affected employees were also working on these unannounced Disney games.</p><p>When it invested in Epic in 2024, Disney wanted to build an entertainment universe, where players could &quot;play, watch, shop and engage with content, characters and stories from Disney, Pixar, Marvel, Star Wars, Avatar, and more.&quot; Epic&#39;s current plans sound less all-encompassing than that, but if they manage to increase engagement with <em>Fortnite</em> and Disney&#39;s brand, that might not matter.<br><br><strong>Update, April 10, 7:29PM ET:</strong> Added a statement from Epic Games.</p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/gaming/epic-is-reportedly-building-an-extraction-shooter-for-disney-220401382.html?src=rss
