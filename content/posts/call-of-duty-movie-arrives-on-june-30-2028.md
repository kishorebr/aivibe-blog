<p>A <em>Call of Duty</em> movie is still happening, but don&#39;t hold your breath for it to hit screens any time soon. Today, the popular FPS&#39; social media <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://x.com/CallofDuty/status/2044830135803928629" data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1">revealed</a> that the movie&#39;s theatrical release date will be June 30, 2028.&nbsp;</p><p> A film adaptation of the game franchise was first <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/entertainment/tv-movies/call-of-duty-is-getting-the-movie-treatment-courtesy-of-paramount-171408410.html" data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1">revealed</a> last year, and shortly after, we <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/entertainment/tv-movies/paramounts-call-of-duty-movie-taps-the-writers-of-yellowstone-and-friday-night-lights-175331091.html" data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1">learned</a> that Taylor Sheridan and Peter Berg would be serving as the producers. The duo, whose past credits include <em>Friday Night Lights</em> and <em>Yellowstone</em>, will also be co-writing the project under Berg&#39;s direction. We still haven&#39;t heard anything about the cast, or even what era of the long-running series will be depicted, so it seems like a safe bet that there&#39;s still a ways to go before this wraps. But CoD is nothing if not a money-maker, so reimagining it as a summer blockbuster seems pretty expected.</p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/entertainment/tv-movies/call-of-duty-movie-arrives-on-june-30-2028-200033481.html?src=rss
