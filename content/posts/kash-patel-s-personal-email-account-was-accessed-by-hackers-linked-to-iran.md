<p>A hacking group called Handala has gained access to FBI Director Kash Patel&#39;s email account, <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.reuters.com/world/us/iran-linked-hackers-claim-breach-of-fbi-directors-personal-email-doj-official-2026-03-27/" data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1"><em>Reuters</em> reports</a>. The group published content from Patel&#39;s email on their website as proof, including photos of Patel &quot;sniffing and smoking cigars&quot; and &quot;making a face while taking a picture of himself in the mirror with a ​large bottle of rum.&quot; </p><p><a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/27/iranian-hackers-claim-breach-of-fbi-director-kash-patels-personal-email-account/" data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1"><em>TechCrunch</em></a> was able to independently confirm that at least some of the emails Handala stole were from Patel&#39;s account by checking information used by mail delivery systems that’s stored in an email&#39;s header. Several stolen emails included a cryptographic signature that linked them to Patel&#39;s account. The FBI has also separately confirmed that the Director&#39;s account was hacked. &quot;The FBI is aware of malicious actors targeting Director Patel&#39;s personal email information, and we have taken all necessary steps to mitigate potential risks associated with this activity,&quot; the Bureau told <em>TechCrunch</em>. &quot;The information in question is historical in nature and involves no government information.&quot;&nbsp;</p><p>The FBI is offering up to $10 million in rewards for more information about the hackers who targeted Patel&#39;s account. Handala presents as a pro-Palestinian hacking group online, but is believed to be one of several aliases used by cyberintelligence units working for the Iranian government, <em>Reuters</em> writes. Groups affiliated with Iran have targeted officials in the US before. In August 2024, the FBI shared that a separate group, APT42, was <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/general/fbi-says-iranian-hackers-are-targeting-both-presidential-campaigns-150019590.html" data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1">trying to gain access</a> to both the Trump and Harris campaigns. Three men associated with APT42 were <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/cybersecurity/three-men-charged-in-connection-with-the-trump-campaign-hack-191154617.html" data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1">later charged</a> that September.</p><p>Handala has appeared to become more active during <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://apnews.com/live/iran-war-israel-trump-03-26-2026" data-i13n="cpos:5;pos:1">the current conflict between the US, Israel and Iran</a>. According to <em>Reuters</em>, the group claimed to be behind <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.fiercebiotech.com/medtech/stryker-continues-bring-hacked-systems-back-online-after-patients-delay-surgeries" data-i13n="cpos:6;pos:1">a cyber attack on Stryker</a>, a medical devices company, earlier in March. Handala also said it accessed and published personal data from Lockheed Martin employees stationed in the Middle East.</p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/cybersecurity/kash-patels-personal-email-account-was-accessed-by-hackers-linked-to-iran-212618474.html?src=rss
