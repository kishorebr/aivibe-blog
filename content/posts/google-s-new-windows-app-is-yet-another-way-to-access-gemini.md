<p>Google has <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://search.google/google-app/desktop/" data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1">introduced</a> a new app for Windows desktops and, unsurprisingly, it puts AI front at center. If you aren&#39;t a big fan of Google&#39;s Gemini chatbot, then skip on past this download. For those of you who are heavy Gemini users, though, this could mean a simpler and more integrated experience on Windows machines.&nbsp;</p><p>Once installed, you can pull up the app&#39;s search bar with the Alt + Space shortcut. Queries typed into this open-ended search box can hunt down information from the web like typical Google search, where AI Mode will be enabled for an extra layer of artificial intelligence for follow-up questions or a deeper dive down a rabbit hole. But the app isn&#39;t limited to web search. It can delve into your computer&#39;s files, other installed apps or Google Drive files to retrieve information. Screen sharing is also built into the app, which enables using Google Lens to conduct AI-powered searches on content displayed on your monitor.&nbsp;</p><p>The app is rolling out globally today in English. Interestingly, this hasn&#39;t been gated to the most recent <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/windows-11-review-microsoft-mindful-os-200028040-200028741.html" data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1">Windows 11</a>, but it does require a machine running at least Windows 10.</p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/apps/googles-new-windows-app-is-yet-another-way-to-access-gemini-214000564.html?src=rss
