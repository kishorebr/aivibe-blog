<p>House Judiciary Committee member Jamie Raskin (D-MD) has asked the US Department of Justice to turn over all its communications with both Apple and Google regarding the companies&#39; decisions to remove apps that shared information about sightings of US Immigrations and Customs Enforcement officers. Several apps that allowed people to share information about where they had seen ICE members were removed from both <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/apps/apple-removes-iceblock-from-the-app-store-after-trump-administrations-demand-031659651.html" data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1">Apple&#39;s App Store</a> and <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/apps/google-is-also-removing-apps-used-to-report-sightings-of-ice-agents-192940612.html" data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1">Google&#39;s Play Store</a> in October. <em>Politico</em> <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.politico.com/news/2026/02/06/raskin-probes-doj-after-apple-google-blocked-ice-tracking-app-00767914" data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1">reported</a> that Raskin has contacted Attorney General Pam Bondi on the issue and also questioned the agency&#39;s use of force against protestors as it executes the immigration policy set by President Donald Trump. </p><p>&quot;The coercion and censorship campaign, which ultimately targets the users of ICE-monitoring applications, is a clear effort to silence this Administration’s critics and suppress any evidence that would expose the Administration’s lies, including its Orwellian attempts to cover up the murders of Renee and Alex,&quot; Raskin wrote to Bondi. He refers to Minneapolis residents Renee Good and Alex Pretti, who were both fatally shot by ICE agents. In the two separate incidents, claims made by federal leaders about the victims and the circumstances of their deaths were <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/big-tech/its-time-for-apple-to-reinstate-iceblock-220802356.html" data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1">contradicted</a> by eyewitnesses or camera footage, echoing <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://chicago.suntimes.com/immigration/2026/02/03/marimar-martinez-shot-border-patrol-chicago-speaks-out-after-good-pretti-deaths-i-am-their-voice" data-i13n="cpos:5;pos:1">violent interactions</a> and <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://abcnews.go.com/US/border-patrol-commander-admitted-lied-tear-gas-incident/story?id=127283392" data-i13n="cpos:6;pos:1">lies about them</a> that occurred while ICE conducted raids in Chicago several months ago. </p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/apps/doj-may-face-investigation-for-pressuring-apple-google-to-remove-apps-for-tracking-ice-agents-212145181.html?src=rss
