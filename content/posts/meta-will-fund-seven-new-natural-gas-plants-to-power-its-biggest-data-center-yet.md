The gas plants will have a combined power output of 5,200 megawatts, and the transmission lines will operate at 500 kilovolts.</p><p>In addition, the company will help fund up to 2,500 MW of new renewable resources. It also agreed to a memorandum of understanding for future nuclear power development. The 4-million-square-foot Richland Parish, LA, data center will be <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/ai/meta-announces-huge-new-data-centers-but-they-could-gobble-up-millions-of-gallons-of-water-per-day-174000478.html" data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1">Meta's biggest yet</a>. It's currently under construction.</p><p>The energy deal follows <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/ai/big-tech-companies-agree-to-not-ruin-your-electric-bill-with-ai-data-centers-230102956.html" data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1">a pledge by tech companies</a>, including Meta, to offset local residents’ <a target="_blank" class="link rapid-with-clickid" href="https://shopping.yahoo.com/rdlw?merchantId=34e37b9c-8975-48da-aa39-df8bcd5badc3&amp;siteId=us-engadget&amp;pageId=1p-autolink&amp;contentUuid=9deb57ab-a95b-44b3-8c83-eb22e1d761ec&amp;featureId=text-link&amp;merchantName=CNBC&amp;linkText=rising+electricity+costs&amp;custData=eyJzb3VyY2VOYW1lIjoiV2ViLURlc2t0b3AtVmVyaXpvbiIsImxhbmRpbmdVcmwiOiJodHRwczovL3d3dy5jbmJjLmNvbS8yMDI2LzAyLzEyL2VsZWN0cmljaXR5LXByaWNlLWRhdGEtY2VudGVyLWFpLWluZmxhdGlvbi1nb2xkbWFuLmh0bWwiLCJjb250ZW50VXVpZCI6IjlkZWI1N2FiLWE5NWItNDRiMy04YzgzLWViMjJlMWQ3NjFlYyIsIm9yaWdpbmFsVXJsIjoiaHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuY25iYy5jb20vMjAyNi8wMi8xMi9lbGVjdHJpY2l0eS1wcmljZS1kYXRhLWNlbnRlci1haS1pbmZsYXRpb24tZ29sZG1hbi5odG1sIn0&amp;signature=AQAAATP_td_hPmmRA-EKcL5Pl1Gt0oxHDjZiazIo7B_aLWeI&amp;gcReferrer=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.cnbc.com%2F2026%2F02%2F12%2Felectricity-price-data-center-ai-inflation-goldman.html" data-i13n="elm:affiliate_link;sellerN:CNBC;elmt:;cpos:4;pos:1" data-original-link="https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/12/electricity-price-data-center-ai-inflation-goldman.html">rising electricity costs</a> from AI data centers. The companies plan to "build, bring or buy the new generation resources and electricity needed to satisfy their new energy demands, paying the full cost of those resources." However, the pledge lacks a binding agreement or any enforcement mechanisms.</p><p>The shift in tone comes in response to growing anger from local communities over the rise of <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/why-do-ai-data-centers-use-so-many-resources-171500010.html" data-i13n="cpos:5;pos:1">power-hungry, environmentally damaging</a> AI data centers. A December <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://navigatorresearch.org/views-of-ai-and-data-centers/" data-i13n="cpos:6;pos:1">poll</a> found that 60 percent of Americans — including majorities of Democrats, Republicans, and independents — support more AI regulation. Just this week, Sen. Bernie Sanders and Rep. Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez introduced a bill to <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/ai/sanders-and-ocasio-cortez-introduce-a-bill-to-pause-us-data-center-construction-174451974.html" data-i13n="cpos:7;pos:1">force a moratorium on data center construction</a> until meaningful regulations are passed.</p><p>We could easily file this and similar moves as Big Tech's latest attempt to convince voters and officials that it can be trusted to do right without enforceable regulations.
