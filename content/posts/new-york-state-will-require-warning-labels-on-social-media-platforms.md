<p>The State of New York will <a data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1" href="https://www.governor.ny.gov/news/governor-hochul-signs-legislation-require-warning-labels-social-media-platforms"><ins>now require</ins></a> social media platforms to display warning labels similar to those found on cigarettes. <a data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1" href="https://legislation.nysenate.gov/pdf/bills/2025/S4505"><ins>The bill</ins></a> was passed by the <a data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1" href="https://www.wxxinews.org/local-news/2025-06-19/new-york-state-lawmakers-pass-bill-to-add-mental-health-warning-label-for-social-media"><ins>New York Legislature in June</ins></a> and signed into law by Gov. Kathy Hochul on Friday. It will apply to any platforms that feature infinite scrolling, auto-play, like counts or algorithmic feeds. The labels will caution those on the platform about potential harm to young users&#39; mental health.</p>
<p>Social media companies will be required to display these warning labels when a user first interacts with any of the features the state considers predatory. The warning will also be displayed periodically after that interaction.&nbsp;</p>
<span id="end-legacy-contents"></span><p>&quot;Keeping New Yorkers safe has been my top priority since taking office, and that includes protecting our kids from the potential harms of social media features that encourage excessive use,&quot; Gov. Hochul <a data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1" href="https://www.governor.ny.gov/news/governor-hochul-signs-legislation-require-warning-labels-social-media-platforms"><ins>said in a statement</ins></a>. The law will apply when any of these platforms are being accessed from New York. Gov. Hochul also <a data-i13n="cpos:5;pos:1" href="https://www.engadget.com/new-york-governor-signs-two-new-bills-into-law-protecting-kids-from-social-media-211935749.html">signed two bills</a> into law last year aimed at protecting kids from social media.</p>
<p>Concerns over the mental health effects of social media platforms on younger users have been mounting and government bodies have been increasingly taking action. A bill similar to the one in New York has been <a data-i13n="cpos:6;pos:1" href="https://www.engadget.com/social-media/a-new-california-bill-would-add-warning-labels-to-social-media-platforms-233653838.html"><ins>proposed in California</ins></a>. This year <a data-i13n="cpos:7;pos:1" href="https://www.engadget.com/social-media/australia-is-adding-twitch-to-its-social-media-ban-for-children-202033276.html"><ins>Australia</ins></a> became the first nation to ban social media for children, with <a data-i13n="cpos:8;pos:1" href="https://www.engadget.com/social-media/denmark-set-to-ban-social-media-for-users-under-15-years-of-age-171602408.html"><ins>Denmark</ins></a> soon to follow.</p>
<p>Last year the US surgeon general <a data-i13n="cpos:9;pos:1" href="https://www.engadget.com/us-surgeon-general-says-that-social-media-like-cigarettes-should-come-with-warning-labels-171005572.html"><ins>said social media</ins></a> should come with warning labels and highlighted <a data-i13n="cpos:10;pos:1" href="https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jamapsychiatry/fullarticle/2749480"><ins>data</ins></a> associating social media use with increased anxiety and depression in youth.
