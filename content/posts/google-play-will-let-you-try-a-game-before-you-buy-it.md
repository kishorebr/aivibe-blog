<p><a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/apps/google-ends-its-30-percent-app-store-fee-and-welcomes-third-party-app-stores-185248647.html" data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1">Google</a> Play has <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://blog.google/products-and-platforms/platforms/google-play/google-play-paid-games-updates/" data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1">introduced</a> a new feature called Game Trials, which will let you play a portion of paid games for free before you commit to buying them. It’s now rolling out to select paid games on mobile, and it’s coming soon to Google Play Games on PC. Titles that offer Game Trials will show a button marked “Try” on their profile pages. When you click it, you’ll see how long you can play the game before you have to buy it. In Google’s example, the survival and horror game <em>Dredge</em> will give you 60 minutes of free play time, after which you’ll get the option to either buy the game or delete it from your device. </p><p>Google has also announced that it’s releasing more paid indie games over the coming months, including <em>Moonlight Peaks, Sledding Game</em> and <em>Low-Budget Repairs</em>. It has launched a new section in the Play store, as well, to feature games optimized for Windows PCs. You can wishlist the games from that section to get a notification when they’re on sale. </p><p>Finally, the company is rolling out Play Games Sidekick, the <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/mobile/google-is-turning-gemini-into-a-gaming-sidekick-with-a-new-android-overlay-130052048.html" data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1">Gemini-powered Android overlay</a> it announced last year, to select games downloaded from Play. Sidekick can show you relevant info and tools for whatever game you&#39;re playing without having to do a search query. But if you’d rather ask other people for gaming advice instead of an AI, you can also look at a game’s Community Posts, a feature now available in English for select titles on their Play pages. </p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/gaming/google-play-will-let-you-try-a-game-before-you-buy-it-051854016.html?src=rss
