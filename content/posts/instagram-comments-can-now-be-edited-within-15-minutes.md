<p>Meta is giving users <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.threads.com/@instagram/post/DW6tJlklmz9?xmt=AQF0U7XK_4Q1l_c8BHKThLQ1IA_yivYEUaiFVUP3CSNYDA" data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1">the ability to edit Instagram comments</a> they leave on posts, though only within a 15 minute window after they&#39;re posted. The setup is similar to how the social app handles editing messages, <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/instagram-finally-lets-you-edit-dms-183412692.html" data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1">an option it first added in 2024</a>, 11 years after direct messaging was <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/2013-12-12-instagram-direct.html" data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1">introduced to Instagram in 2013</a>.</p><p>You&#39;re only able to edit comments you left with your own account, and the process of actually doing so is easy. Just tap on the word &quot;Edit&quot; under your comment to pull up a text box where you can tweak, rework or embellish what you&#39;ve written, and then press the blue check mark to save it. Meta says comments can be edited as many times as you want in that 15 minute window, so if you need to make more changes, you have that option.</p><p>Comments can show up in multiple ways across Instagram — <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/social-media/instagram-stories-are-getting-comments-183051677.html" data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1">including Stories</a>, as of 2024 — so offering a way to edit them is a welcome addition. The new option is just the latest in a series of changes Meta has introduced to the social app in the last month. Earlier in March, the company announced that it was <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/social-media/meta-is-killing-end-to-end-encryption-in-instagram-dms-195207421.html" data-i13n="cpos:5;pos:1">removing end-to-end encryption</a> from Instagram DMs. At the end of the month, Meta also began testing <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/social-media/meta-is-testing-an-instagram-plus-subscription-service-with-exclusive-features-181215180.html" data-i13n="cpos:6;pos:1">Instagram Plus</a>, a subscription service that unlocks new features for the app&#39;s Stories feature.</p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/social-media/instagram-comments-can-now-be-edited-within-15-minutes-195000640.html?src=rss
