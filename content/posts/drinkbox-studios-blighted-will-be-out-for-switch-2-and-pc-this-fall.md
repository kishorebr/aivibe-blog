<p>During today&#39;s <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/gaming/nintendo/nintendo-just-announced-an-indie-world-showcase-for-tomorrow-at-9am-et-164706666.html" data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1">Nintendo Indie World presentation</a>, we got a narrower time frame for when and where we&#39;ll be able to play the next project from Drinkbox Studios. The team announced that <em>Blighted</em> will be coming out in this fall for both the Nintendo Switch 2 and for PC.&nbsp;It’ll arrive on the same date for both platforms, but we don’t have any more specific timeline than the season.</p><p>We <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/gaming/blighted-is-a-trippy-metroidvania-from-the-team-behind-guacamelee-000030687.html" data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1">first heard about</a> <em>Blighted</em> during last year&#39;s Summer Games Fest. With past credits such as <em>Guacamelee!</em>, <em>Guacamelee! 2</em> and <em>Severed</em>, the studio is now turning its eye toward a 3D Metroidvania-RPG. It may be a new direction in genre, but Drinkbox&#39;s signature moody, eerie vibes do seem to be returning in heaps for <em>Blighted</em>. The core premise of the game revolves around the player regaining lost communal memories and also eating brains, so it’s definitely heavy on the surreal and slightly macabre. <em>Blighted</em> will also have options for both solo and co-op play of its story. </p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/gaming/drinkbox-studios-blighted-will-be-out-for-switch-2-and-pc-this-fall-203638261.html?src=rss
