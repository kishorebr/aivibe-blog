<p class="wp-block-paragraph">OpenAI is already shutting down ChatGPT Atlas, its browser that could do tasks for you on your behalf, less than a year after launching it. Atlas was announced <a href="https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/803475/openais-ai-powered-browser-chatgpt-atlas-google-chrome-competition-agent">in October</a>, but as part of its wave of news <a href="https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/963464/openai-gpt-5-6-codex-chatgpt-work">about ChatGPT Work today</a>, the company confirmed that it will be "sunsetting" Atlas and is targeting an August 9th date for deprecation.</p>
<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The shutdown follows OpenAI's push to reduce "side quests" and catch up with Anthropic <a href="https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/911118/openai-memo-cro-ai-competition-anthropic">on productivity features</a>. As part of that push, in March, <em>The Wall Street Journal</em> <a href="https://archive.is/20260319230410/https://www.wsj.com/tech/openai-plans-launch-of-desktop-superapp-to-refocus-simplify-user-experience-9e19931d">reported that</a> OpenAI planned to combine the ChatGPT app, Codex, and Atlas into a desktop "superapp" - ChatGPT Work appears to be the result …</p>
