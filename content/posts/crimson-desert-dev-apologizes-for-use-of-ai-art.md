<p class="has-text-align-none">Reviews of <em>Crimson Desert</em> <a href="https://gamerant.com/crimson-desert-review/">have</a> <a href="https://www.ign.com/articles/crimson-desert-review">been</a> <a href="https://gameinformer.com/review/crimson-desert/open-world-overload">mixed</a>, but the bigger issue for the game has been the discovery of what <a href="https://www.reddit.com/r/PS5/comments/1rz5qfb/crimson_desert_appears_to_use_generative_ai/">appeared</a> to be <a href="https://www.ign.com/articles/crimson-desert-players-think-theyve-found-ai-generated-art-in-game">AI-generated assets</a> in the <a href="https://www.reddit.com/r/CrimsonDesert/comments/1rz2f2l/found_this_ai_painting/?utm_source=embedv2&amp;utm_medium=post_embed&amp;utm_content=post_body&amp;embed_host_url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.ign.com%2Farticles%2Fcrimson-desert-players-think-theyve-found-ai-generated-art-in-game">final</a> release. Now the developer has acknowledged that AI art was indeed used during the game's creation, but says that it was intended to be replaced before release.  In a <a href="https://x.com/crimsondesert_/status/2035718975909470706?s=46">statement on X</a>, the company said it was conducting a "comprehensive audit" to identify and replace any AI-generated content. </p>
<p class="has-text-align-none">The company apologized for both its inclusion in the final release and for not being more transparent about its use during development. "We should have clearly disclosed our use of AI," it said.</p>
