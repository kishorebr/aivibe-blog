<p>Ivy Road, the video game developer behind <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/gaming/how-to-watch-annapurnas-publisher-showcase-on-february-24-193539718.html" data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1">Annapurna-published</a> cozy game <em>Wanderstop</em>, is <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.ivyroad.fun/studioupdates/ivy-road-march-update" data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1">shutting down</a> on March 31. While <em>Wanderstop</em> was well-received and even critically acclaimed, it seems like it wasn’t enough of a hit to sustain the studio while it develops a new game without getting investors involved. In its announcement, the Ivy Road team said the company failed to land a funding and publishing deal for its new project, <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://bsky.app/profile/lizc3d.bsky.social/post/3mah23rqca22o" data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1"><em>Engine Angel</em>.</a> The studio’s problems securing funding for its new game first came to light back in January when it <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://bsky.app/profile/ivyroadgames.bsky.social/post/3mdnvnte3v223" data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1">laid off</a> five team members. </p><p>Even though the studio is shutting down soon, the team said it has one last surprise that will help <em>Wanderstop</em> reach new players. It didn’t say what the surprise was, but the team said Annapurna Interactive will share more news about it in the future. <em>Wanderstop</em> revolves around a former fighter, Alta, who manages a tea shop in a magical forest. In the game, you can gather ingredients and concoct tea, tidy up, talk to customers and learn their stories or just sit on a bench to think and relax. The game will still be available to play and purchase even after Ivy Road shuts down. </p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/gaming/wanderstop-developer-ivy-road-is-shutting-down-153655278.html?src=rss
