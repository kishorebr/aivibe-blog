<p>Publisher and game management platform <a data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1" href="http://indie.io/"><ins>indie.io</ins></a> just announced the pending launch of <a data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1" href="https://www.indiepass.com/"><ins>something called Indie Pass</ins></a>. This is a subscription service, so it&#39;s sort of like Game Pass but for indie titles.</p>
<p>It launches on April 13 and will offer over 70 games on that date, with more coming down the line. Not a single person on this planet wants another monthly subscription to manage, but this one costs just $8. That&#39;s a pretty good deal, considering Game Pass Ultimate<a data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1" href="https://www.engadget.com/microsoft-jacks-the-price-of-game-pass-ultimate-up-to-30-a-month-142441307.html"><ins> costs a whopping $30 per month</ins></a>.</p>
<span id="end-legacy-contents"></span><div id="2c8c3b0faaa94acbb2b7ec46102aac63"><iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/iosSN_0U7Z8?si=Tqdb6PU5O9Z7bbuu" title="YouTube video player" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></div>
<p>However, these subscription platforms are only as good as their libraries. The company has already confirmed a bunch of nifty titles like the cozy game <em>Echoes of the Plum Grove</em>, the farm-based shooter <em>Air Hares</em> and the tactical RPG <em>Dark Deity</em>. It also promises a &quot;constantly evolving catalog.&quot; Indie.io publishes a lot of stuff, so that should make it easier to keep the catalog stacked.</p>
<p>The company is also currently <a data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1" href="https://www.indiepass.com/developers"><ins>courting indie developers and publishers</ins></a>, with a promise to reveal some of these partnerships in the coming weeks. This could be a nice way to drum up interest in new or smaller games, but everything really depends on if people are willing to pony up for another subscription.</p>
<p>Indie Pass is just for PC. However, there doesn&#39;t seem to be anything that would prevent the platform from working on a Steam Deck via the console&#39;s Proton layer. This lets players run Windows-specific titles on the console&#39;s Linux-based OS. This has long been considered a good way to run indie.io-published games that don&#39;t make their way to Steam. Engadget has reached out to the company for specifics and will update this post when we hear back.</p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/gaming/pc/indie-pass-is-a-forthcoming-subscription-service-exclusively-for-indie-games-171304359.html?src=rss
