<p>The next time you check your internet speed or whether your favorite site is down, another company may be profiting. On Tuesday, Ziff Davis <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://investor.ziffdavis.com/news/news-details/2026/Ziff-Davis-Announces-Definitive-Agreement-to-Sell-Connectivity-Division-to-Accenture/default.aspx" data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1">announced</a> that it sold its Connectivity division, which includes Ookla&#39;s Speedtest and Downdetector, to Accenture. The deal is worth $1.2 billion in cash.</p><p>According to <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.reuters.com/business/ziff-davis-sell-connectivity-division-accenture-12-billion-2026-03-03/" data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1"><em>Reuters</em></a>, the deal will allow Ziff Davis to focus on its core brands, including <em>IGN</em>, <em>Mashable </em>and <em>Everyday Health</em>. Brand consolidation appears to be the name of the game, as the company recently <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.videogameschronicle.com/news/games-media-set-for-more-layoffs-as-ign-owned-eurogamer-cuts-editorial-staff/" data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1">laid off</a> some of <em>Eurogamer</em>&#39;s most experienced editors and its entire video team. That followed a rotation of editorial staff among Ziff Davis&#39;s other gaming publications, which reduced <em>VG247</em> to a small, two-person gaming guides site.</p><p>Ziff Davis bought Ookla for $15 million in 2014. <em>Reuters</em> notes that the division seized on the 5G rollout and a surge in pandemic-driven bandwidth demand to boost its impressive return on the investment. (Connectivity generated a whopping $231 million in 2025.)</p><p>Accenture is a global technology consulting company headquartered in Dublin. The company <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://newsroom.accenture.com/news/2026/accenture-to-acquire-ookla-to-strengthen-network-intelligence-and-experience-with-data-and-ai-for-enterprises" data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1">views</a> the acquisition as a key step in building &quot;end-to-end network intelligence services essential for AI-based transformation.&quot; (Can someone who speaks corporate translate, please?) The deal may take a few months to be finalized, and Ziff Davis will continue to operate Speedtest and Downdetector in the meantime.</p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/cybersecurity/downdetector-and-speedtest-have-been-sold-for-over-1-billion-201741894.html?src=rss
