<p>A recent Amazon Web Services (AWS) outage that lasted 13 hours was reportedly caused by one of its own AI tools, <a data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1" href="https://www.ft.com/content/00c282de-ed14-4acd-a948-bc8d6bdb339d"><ins>according to reporting by </ins><em><ins>Financial Times</ins></em></a>. This happened in December after engineers deployed the Kiro AI coding tool to make certain changes, say four people familiar with the matter.</p>
<p>Kiro is an agentic tool, meaning it can take autonomous actions on behalf of users. In this case, the bot reportedly determined that it needed to &quot;delete and recreate the environment.&quot; This is what allegedly led to the lengthy outage that primarily impacted China.</p>
