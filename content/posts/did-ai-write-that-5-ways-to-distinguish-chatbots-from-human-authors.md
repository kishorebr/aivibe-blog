ZDNET's key takeaways  AI models follow certain structural rules when generating text.This can make it easier to identify their writing.They tend towards contrasts, for example: "It's not X -- it's Y."The past few years have seen a flood of AI-generated text wash over the internet. As the models behind this text improve, so too does their ability to imitate the intricacies of human speech; at the same time, our methods for detecting it have been improving, and there's been an active online dialogue about some of the most common quirks of AI-generated text.Historically, one of the more well-known tells of ChatGPT, for example, has been the chatbot's fondness for em dashes.
