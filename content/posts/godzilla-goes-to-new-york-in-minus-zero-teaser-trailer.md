<p>Japanese entertainment company Toho has <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://godzilla.com/pages/godzilla-minus-zero?srsltid=AfmBOooOQBbDw5OTAjTzQPkcdWGBlTOnhhxv2Flz5vHjXDGJwdikX8J1" data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1">released</a> a teaser video for <em>Godzilla Minus Zero</em>, the upcoming sequel to the award-winning film <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/you-can-now-watch-godzilla-minus-one-at-home-on-netflix-194759870.html" data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1"><em>Godzilla Minus One</em></a>. The teaser shows the famous monster next to the Statue of Liberty as it rampages across New York. <em>Godzilla Minus Zero</em> is set in 1949, two years after the events of the first film, and will be a direct sequel. You’ll see familiar faces from <em>Minus One</em> in the short trailer, as well, namely Koichi Shikishima and Noriko Oishi, two of the first movie’s main characters.</p><p>The kaiju flick was filmed specifically for IMAX with high-definition digital cameras. Even its audio was optimized for the massive screen’s immersive cinema experience. <em>Minus One</em> won an Oscar for Best Visual Effects, so expectations are high for this sequel.
