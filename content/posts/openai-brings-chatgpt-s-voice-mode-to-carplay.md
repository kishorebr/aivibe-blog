<p>In a <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://x.com/OpenAI/status/2039748699350532097/photo/1" data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1">surprise release</a>, OpenAI has made ChatGPT&#39;s Voice mode available through Apple CarPlay. If you&#39;re running the latest version of both <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/mobile/ios-264-is-here-with-playlist-playground-and-new-emoji-171343120.html" data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1">iOS</a> and the ChatGPT app, and own a CarPlay-compatible vehicle, you can check out the experience. To get started, download all the necessary software, connect your iPhone to CarPlay and select &quot;New voice chat&quot; from ChatGPT.  When the in-app text indicates ChatGPT is &quot;listening,&quot; you can start a conversation.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</p><p>There are some notable limitations to using ChatGPT Voice with CarPlay. For one, OpenAI&#39;s chatbot can&#39;t control car functions. If you want to adjust the cabin temperature or skip tracks, you&#39;ll still need Siri for those tasks. Due to Apple&#39;s restrictions, you also can&#39;t start using ChatGPT through a wake word like you can Siri. For example, to resume a previous conversation,&nbsp;you need to open the ChatGPT app from CarPlay and tap a recent or pinned chat.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p><p>With those limitations in mind, OpenAI suggests you can use Voice mode to get how-to advice, brainstorm ideas and practice languages. Personally, I like to listen to podcasts and music when I&#39;m driving, but if talking with ChatGPT is your thing, you do you. &nbsp; &nbsp;</p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/ai/openai-brings-chatgpts-voice-mode-to-carplay-191422297.html?src=rss
