<p class="has-text-align-none">After Sony drew some <a href="https://www.theverge.com/android/930459/sony-these-photos-look-terrible">unwanted attention</a> for a post demonstrating its AI Camera Assistant on the <a href="https://www.theverge.com/tech/927928/sony-xperia-viii-release-date-price-specs">Xperia 1 XIII</a>, it's trying to clarify how the feature works. The company says it doesn't edit photos, but makes suggestions based on lighting, depth, and subject. Point the camera at something, and it will give you four options for changing exposure, color, and background blur. </p>
<p class="has-text-align-none">In its product video, Sony says that the AI Camera Assistant will also suggest "the most photogenic angle." Though the clip only shows it suggesting that someone zoom in, which is not the same as suggesting a camera angle. </p>
