<p>Lenovo’s buzziest gaming-related MWC announcement is undoubtedly its foldable Legion Go handheld-cum-laptop hybrid, which you can read all about <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/gaming/pc/the-lenovo-legion-go-fold-gaming-handheld-concept-looks-awkward-but-its-versatility-is-endless-230000816.html" data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1">here</a>. But that wasn’t all the company showed off. We’re also getting an updated Legion Tab, Lenovo’s powerful gaming tablet.</p><p>Like the Gen 4 and 3 models before it, the latest Legion Tab features an 8.8-inch LCD display with an 165Hz refresh rate. The 3K panel can get up to 600 nits of brightness. The notable spec boosts come in the form of a jump up to a Snapdragon 8 Gen 5 chipset and a larger 9,000mAh battery, which should help you power through those long-haul flight gaming sessions.</p><p>The 360g Gen 5 Legion Tab is equipped with 16GB of RAM and up to 512GB of storage. It also features what looks like an RGB light ring next to the rear camera cutout (just in case anyone needs reminding that you’re a capital G on-the-go gamer), and two USB-C ports.</p><p>The latest Legion Tab also supports Lenovo AI Engine+, which uses AI for audio enhancement and noise cancellation. Available in Eclipse Black, Glacier White and Surge, it will be available to buy in May, with prices starting at $849.</p><p>Lenovo also unveiled a new 15-inch variant of its Gen 11 Legion 7a gaming laptop, after announcing an updated 16-inch model at <a target="_blank" rel="" class="rapid-with-clickid link" href="https://shopping.yahoo.com/rdlw?siteId=us-engadget&amp;pageId=1p-autolink&amp;contentUuid=f9d48ae7-c542-493b-94b4-387b29476601&amp;featureId=text-link&amp;linkText=CES&amp;custData=eyJzb3VyY2VOYW1lIjoiV2ViLURlc2t0b3AtVmVyaXpvbiIsImxhbmRpbmdVcmwiOiJodHRwczovL25ld3MubGVub3ZvLmNvbS9wcmVzc3Jvb20vcHJlc3MtcmVsZWFzZXMvbGVub3ZvLWxldmVscy11cC1jZXMtd2l0aC1sZWdpb24tZ2FtaW5nLWRldmljZXMvIiwiY29udGVudFV1aWQiOiJmOWQ0OGFlNy1jNTQyLTQ5M2ItOTRiNC0zODdiMjk0NzY2MDEiLCJvcmlnaW5hbFVybCI6Imh0dHBzOi8vbmV3cy5sZW5vdm8uY29tL3ByZXNzcm9vbS9wcmVzcy1yZWxlYXNlcy9sZW5vdm8tbGV2ZWxzLXVwLWNlcy13aXRoLWxlZ2lvbi1nYW1pbmctZGV2aWNlcy8ifQ&amp;signature=AQAAAb2X1tWLpdpKuqGJwsc4kGiI5610Yb0eP-CZplNXKz0F&amp;gcReferrer=https%3A%2F%2Fnews.lenovo.com%2Fpressroom%2Fpress-releases%2Flenovo-levels-up-ces-with-legion-gaming-devices%2F" data-i13n="elm:affiliate_link;sellerN:;elmt:;slk:CES;cpos:2;pos:1" data-original-link="https://news.lenovo.com/pressroom/press-releases/lenovo-levels-up-ces-with-legion-gaming-devices/">CES</a> in January.
