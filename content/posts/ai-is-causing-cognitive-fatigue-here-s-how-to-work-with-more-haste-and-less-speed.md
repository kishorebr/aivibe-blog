    AI is causing cognitive fatigue. Here's how to work with more haste and less speed
     
    Research suggests people are working harder and not smarter with AI, but there are ways to turn emerging tech into a valuable tool.
      Written by 
            Mark Samuels, Senior ContributorSenior Contributor  June 3, 2026 at 11:02 a.m. PT                            S847/iStock / Getty Images PlusFollow ZDNET: Add us as a preferred source on Google. Harvard Business Review found that AI doesn't reduce work; it intensifies it, leading to cognitive fatigue and unsustainable hours.While the common perception is that AI can help reduce workloads, allowing employees to focus more on higher-value and more engaging tasks, HBR's research found that staff using AI worked more quickly and often ended up with more to do, not less.
