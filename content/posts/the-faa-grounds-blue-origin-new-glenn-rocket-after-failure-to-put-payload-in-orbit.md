<p>The Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) has grounded Blue Origin&#39;s New Glenn rocket following an incident during Sunday&#39;s launch from Cape Canaveral, <a data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1" href="https://www.orlandosentinel.com/2026/04/20/faa-grounds-blue-origin-new-glenn-after-labeling-mission-a-mishap/"><ins>according to reporting by </ins><em><ins>Orlando Sentinel</ins></em></a> and others. The rocket looked good on the way up but was ultimately unable to <a data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1" href="https://www.engadget.com/science/space/blue-origin-landed-its-recycled-new-glenn-booster-but-failed-to-put-payload-in-orbit-055846419.html"><ins>put its payload into the correct orbit</ins></a>.</p>
<p>The FAA is calling the incident a &quot;mishap&quot; and is beginning an investigation to &quot;enhance public safety, determine the root cause of the event and identify corrective actions to avoid it from happening again.&quot; The organization said in a statement that a &quot;return to flight is based on the FAA determining that any system, process or procedure related to the mishap does not affect public safety.&quot;</p>
<span id="end-legacy-contents"></span><p>The company hasn&#39;t provided any information as to what happened with New Glenn that made it mess up the positioning. It was supposed to position a satellite into a 285 mile orbit after completing two burns, but telemetry data shows that the satellite only reached a 95 mile orbit, which is not sustainable.</p>
<div id="ee8167b73cc84bdc938f3435369f3b80"><iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/ew-rXo0FWhA?si=Otcol_1J_joozmJj" title="YouTube video player" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></div>
<p>This was New Glenn&#39;s third mission, and not the first time the rocket has been grounded by the FAA. Blue Origin was unable to land it after the <a data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1" href="https://www.engadget.com/science/space/blue-origins-new-glenn-rocket-launches-into-orbit-on-its-maiden-flight-073451555.html"><ins>debut launch</ins></a> and it wasn&#39;t allowed to fly again <a data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1" href="https://www.engadget.com/science/space/blue-origins-new-glenn-rocket-safely-made-it-to-space-a-second-time-230324439.html"><ins>for nearly three months</ins></a>. The agency hasn&#39;t announced when the rocket will be cleared to fly this time, so we don&#39;t know if it will put a crimp in Blue Origin&#39;s plans to launch a bunch of Amazon Leo broadband satellites. That mission is <a data-i13n="cpos:5;pos:1" href="https://www.engadget.com/big-tech/amazons-satellite-internet-service-is-scheduled-for-mid-2026-availability-164046305.html"><ins>currently scheduled for later this year</ins></a> and will use the New Glenn rocket.</p>
<p>The FAA has grounded several rockets due to mishaps in recent years, including the smaller <a data-i13n="cpos:6;pos:1" href="https://aiaa.org/2022/09/14/faa-grounds-blue-origin-new-shepard-rocket-pending-investigation-of-aborted-launch/"><ins>Blue Origin New Shepard</ins></a>. It has also grounded <a data-i13n="cpos:7;pos:1" href="https://www.space.com/space-exploration/launches-spacecraft/spacex-grounds-its-falcon-9-rocket-after-a-problem-with-its-upper-stage-will-the-crew-12-astronaut-mission-be-affected"><ins>SpaceX&#39;s Falcon 9</ins></a> and <a data-i13n="cpos:8;pos:1" href="https://www.engadget.com/faa-ends-spacex-starship-sn8-sn9-investiations-220025441.html"><ins>Starship</ins></a>.</p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/science/space/the-faa-grounds-blue-origin-new-glenn-rocket-after-failure-to-put-payload-in-orbit-173016117.html?src=rss
