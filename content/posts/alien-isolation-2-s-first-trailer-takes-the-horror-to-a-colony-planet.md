                Time works differently in space and on other planets, so the decade-plus that Alien Isolation fans have been waiting for the sequel might have felt like a blink of an eye on a different world. On terra firma, though, it's been a 12-year wait. Now, following an initial teaser last month, we got our first proper look at Alien Isolation 2 during Summer Game Fest's main showcase by way of a cinematic trailer.
The brief clip features a station in disarray, a flamethrower, a scream and a Xenomorph. All the correct elements, then. According to developers Creative Assembly, the survivor horror sequel is actually set on "a remote, storm-ravaged colony-world," giving the alien much more space to stalk its prey. Instead of being cooped up on a spaceship, players will "navigate the planet's surface, and explore the claustrophobic confines of the Weyland-Yutani outpost of Kurosaki Station." So, maybe not that much more space.
