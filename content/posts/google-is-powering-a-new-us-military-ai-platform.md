<p class="has-text-align-none">The Department of Defense is announcing its own "bespoke" AI platform, <a href="https://genai.mil/">GenAI.mil</a>, and Google Cloud's Gemini will be the first AI tool available on it, <a href="https://www.war.gov/News/Releases/Release/Article/4354916/the-war-department-unleashes-ai-on-new-genaimil-platform/">according to a press release</a>.</p>
<p class="has-text-align-none">Secretary of Defense Pete Hegseth (who has dubbed himself Secretary of War, though the name has not been <a href="https://www.military.com/feature/2025/10/17/department-of-war-not-legally-what-trumps-executive-order-really-does.html">legally changed by Congress</a>) promised that the platform "puts the worlds [sic] most powerful frontier AI models directly into the hands of every American warrior" and will "make our fighting force more lethal than ever before." In a video, Hegseth says that "the future of American warfare is here, and it's spelled A-I." </p>
