<p>Resident Evil legend Shinji Mikami&#39;s new studio, Unbound Inc., has been acquired by Shift Up, the company behind <em>Stellar Blade</em> and <em>Goddess of Victory: Nikke</em>. Unbound&#39;s unannounced games will be fully supported and distributed by South Korean publisher Shift Up, which is led by CEO Hyung-Tae Kim.</p>
<p>Mikami is an icon of Japanese horror as the director of <em>Resident Evil</em>, its 2002 remake and <em>Resident Evil 4</em>, as well as a founder of PlatinumGames and Tango Gameworks. Tango was responsible for The Evil Within series, <em>Ghostwire Tokyo</em> and <em>Hi-Fi Rush</em>. Shift Up recently developed the hit action game <em>Stellar Blade</em>, with Kim as director.</p>
<span id="end-legacy-contents"></span><p>Even with an <a data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PTColDTq19M">adorably grotesque introduction video</a>, it&#39;s unclear exactly what Unbound is working on at the moment, but the studio is targeting the global PC and console market. Its concepts involve plenty of monsters, as is tradition.</p>
<div id="2b27437dc7654718b25738ead54ab3d0"><iframe src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/PTColDTq19M?rel=0" style="top:0;left:0;width:100%;height:100%;position:absolute;border:0;" allowfullscreen scrolling="no" data-embed-domain="www.youtube.com"></iframe></div>
<p>Consider even the surface-level possibilities here: The campy horror of Resident Evil blended with the melodramatic beauty of <em>Stellar Blade. </em>The frenzy of <em>Hi-Fi Rush</em> amped up by the anime stylings of <em>Goddess of Victory: Nikke</em>. <em>The Evil Within III</em>, but make it sexy. These are jokes, but the sentiment remains — this partnership makes a lot of sense and it&#39;ll be exciting to see what shakes out.</p>
<p>“We believe we can respect each other as creators and make games together,” Mikami said in a <a data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1" href="https://shiftup.co.kr/eng/news/blog.php?ptype=view&amp;idx=414&amp;page=1&amp;code=blog_eng">Shift Up blog post</a> about the deal. “And I think with Hyung-Tae, we can even enjoy the hard parts.... Seeing my own vision and ideals come into focus like this, and finding someone whose direction aligns so closely is something I’ve rarely experienced before in my career. I hope we keep building together for a long time.”</p>
<p>It&#39;s also heartening to see stability for Mikami&#39;s new studio. His previous team, Tango Gameworks, was acquired by Microsoft in 2021, and Mikami left in 2023 after the release of <em>Hi-Fi Rush</em>. <a data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1" href="https://www.engadget.com/arkane-austin-and-tango-gameworks-have-been-shut-down-183913558.html">Microsoft shuttered Tango</a> in 2024 during a period of <a data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1" href="https://www.engadget.com/layoffs-are-sucking-the-joy-out-of-video-games--this-weeks-gaming-news-174541450.html">mass game industry layoffs</a>, and its remaining team was eventually sold to Krafton. Mikami has been quietly building up his own studio since 2022.</p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/gaming/mr-resident-evil-signs-a-deal-with-mr-stellar-blade-180158872.html?src=rss
