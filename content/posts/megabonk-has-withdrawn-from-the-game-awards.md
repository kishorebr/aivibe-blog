<p>Nominees for The Game Awards were <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/gaming/the-game-awards-2025-nominees-include-clair-obscur-hollow-knight-silksong-and-hades-2-173626649.html" data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1">released</a> yesterday, and there&#39;s a whole lot of indie excellence on display. However, one notable contender among them has decided to withdraw from contention.&nbsp;</p><p>The fabulously titled <em>Megabonk</em> received a nod for the Best Debut Indie Game category. </div><p>The category is kind of a weird one, since indie creators may have worked at other big or small studios. Even fully self-taught devs will have made and maybe even released several projects before having any kind of breakthrough success or popularity. But if vedinad feels the game doesn&#39;t fit the category, then it&#39;s still a gracious move to bow out.&nbsp;</p><p><em>Megabonk</em> follows in the recent lineage of <em>Vampire Survivors</em>, a popular bullet-hell roguelike that just arrived in <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/ar-vr/vampire-survivors-vr-asks-what-if-the-bullet-hell-was-on-your-face-190749256.html" data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1">virtual reality</a>. The Megabonk riff takes the 2D concept into a low-poly 3D and adds some pretty wacky characters into the mix. Venidad said the title sold <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://x.com/MegabonkGame/status/1973773917040111965" data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1">1 million copies</a> in just two weeks, making it one of the latest Steam indie sensations to have a big moment.</p><p></p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/gaming/megabonk-has-withdrawn-from-the-game-awards-212822746.html?src=rss
