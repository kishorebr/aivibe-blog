<p>The indie gaming news has been <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/gaming/nintendo/blue-prince-is-out-on-switch-2-today-complete-with-mouse-control-162242506.html" data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1">flying</a> <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/gaming/nintendo/two-fantastic-indie-games-not-named-blue-prince-also-just-arrived-on-switch-182648197.html" data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1">out</a> of Nintendo&#39;s event today at a rapid-fire pace. One of the announcements came from rhythm game <em>Ratatan</em>, which will release on July 16. The title will be arriving that day on the Nintendo Switch 2, PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X/S. It&#39;s already been available on PC <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/gaming/pc/ratatan-a-spiritual-successor-to-patapon-hits-early-access-on-september-19-171119266.html" data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1">since September</a> as an early access game.&nbsp;</p><p>Unfortunately, readers may notice that the original Switch isn&#39;t on that list, which is not an oversight. &quot;At this time, we have determined that the multiplayer experience on Nintendo Switch has not yet reached the quality level we aim to deliver,&quot; the team said in a <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://store.steampowered.com/news/app/2949320/view/496095786926343291?l=english" data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1">post</a> on Steam. &quot;As a result, we have decided not to proceed with a Nintendo Switch version for now.&quot;</p><p>We first <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/gaming/ratatan-demands-your-attention-rhythmically-160048067.html" data-i13n="cpos:5;pos:1">got our hands on</a> <em>Ratatan</em> during Summer Games Fest 2025. Although the studio of Ratata Arts may not sound familiar, this group includes several devs from team that created the Patapon games, a series that began in 2007. That lineage is clear in the core concept and in the slightly brain-breaking level of attention required to keep your little army of Cobuns in sync and on beat. <em>Ratatan</em> brings some roguelite and RPG customization into the mix along with the familiar rhythm mechanics.</p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/gaming/ratatan-is-marching-its-way-into-your-console-library-on-july-16-214540775.html?src=rss
