<p>It&#39;s happening. Ubisoft has scheduled a livestream for April 23 at 12PM ET to discuss the long-awaited <em>Assassin&#39;s Creed Black Flag</em> remake. The showcase will be available to watch on the company&#39;s <a data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7hkFaTypP1Q"><ins>YouTube</ins></a> and <a data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1" href="https://www.twitch.tv/ubisoft"><ins>Twitch</ins></a> pages.</p>
<p>It&#39;s officially called <em>Assassin&#39;s Creed Black Flag Resynced</em> and has <a data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1" href="https://www.ign.com/articles/ubisoft-ceo-confirms-multiple-assassins-creed-remakes-are-on-the-way"><ins>been rumored to be in development for years</ins></a>. Ubisoft ended speculation by <a data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1" href="https://www.engadget.com/gaming/ubisoft-confirms-assassins-creed-black-flag-remake-193629862.html"><ins>announcing the game last month</ins></a>.</p>

<p>We don&#39;t know anything about how the game will play or look, as Ubisoft has only dropped some promotional art featuring protagonist Edward Kenway lounging on a boat. The livestream should feature a trailer that will answer many burning questions.</p>
<p>For instance, rumors have been swirling that this is a <a data-i13n="cpos:5;pos:1" href="https://www.ign.com/articles/ubisoft-finally-confirms-assassins-creed-black-flag-resynced-the-remake-we-all-knew-was-coming"><ins>total top-to-bottom remake</ins></a> and not a simple port. That makes sense given the <a data-i13n="cpos:6;pos:1" href="https://www.engadget.com/2013-10-29-metareview-assassins-creed-4-black-flag.html"><ins>continued popularity of </ins><em><ins>Black Flag</ins></em></a>. It&#39;s also been rumored that this new version will <a data-i13n="cpos:7;pos:1" href="https://www.ign.com/articles/stop-removing-the-modern-day-assassins-creed-fans-react-as-black-flag-remake-reportedly-cuts-everything-outside-the-animus"><ins>cut out all of the modern day gameplay sections</ins></a>, with a total focus on pirate-themed action.</p>
