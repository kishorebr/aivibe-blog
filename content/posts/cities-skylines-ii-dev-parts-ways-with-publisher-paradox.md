<p>Game publisher Paradox Interactive has <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://forum.paradoxplaza.com/forum/threads/an-update-on-cities-skylines-ii.1873154/" data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1">split</a> with Colossal Order, the studio behind <em>Cities: Skylines II</em>. Colossal Order, which also made the original <em>Cities: Skylines</em> back in 2015, will move on to something new after completing a few last updates. The intellectual property will remain with Paradox, which said today that work on <em>Cities: Skylines II</em> will be moved to Iceflake Studios. The Finland-based internal team will be responsible for all development, including future content updates and the long-awaited console version, starting in 2026.</p><p>The move comes after about two years of trying to salvage the citybuilding sequel and the reputations of all parties. While the game was <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/cities-skylines-ii-will-arrive-on-pc-and-consoles-october-24th-2023-181911885.html" data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1">initially</a> expected to debut in October 2023 on PC and consoles, the launch was limited to PC, with higher minimum specs than previously announced and with a lot of bugs. The ongoing issues caused <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/whats-up-with-the-toxicity-around-cities-skylines-ii-213034938.html" data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1">tension and toxicity</a> between Colossal Order and the <em>Cities: Skylines</em> audience. Although the game has reached a more stable state, new content has been <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/gaming/cities-skylines-ii-delays-bridges--ports-dlc-to-q4-200052345.html" data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1">slow</a> to arrive and there is still no sign of a console launch as the studio exits their project.</p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/gaming/cities-skylines-ii-dev-parts-ways-with-publisher-paradox-210229692.html?src=rss
