<p>YouTube is experiencing an outage across the United States, with users in other countries like Canada, India, the Philippines, Australia and Russia also <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.reddit.com/r/youtube/comments/1r7onh1/youtube_went_down_800pm/" data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1">having problems</a> with accessing the website. The issue seems to have started at around 8 PM Eastern time and reached 338,000 reports on <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://downdetector.com/status/youtube/" data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1">Downdetector</a> before starting to taper down. More users reported having issues accessing the app, but I personally lost access to the web homepage first. </p><p>As of 9:22 PM, users are still reporting being unable to access YouTube on <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.reddit.com/r/youtube/comments/1r7onh1/youtube_went_down_800pm/?sort=new" data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1">Reddit</a>. As of 9:33 PM, users are complaining that they still can’t access the service, though others say it’s back up for them. Some people are reporting partial restoration of service, with the homepage now being accessible but not seeing any recommended videos. </p><p>Downdetector also got thousands of reports of <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://downdetector.com/status/google/" data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1">Google</a> being down at around 8 PM Eastern time. As of 9:53 PM, Engadget Managing Editor Cherlynn Low reports that both YouTube and Google Home Assistant are still inaccessible for her. As of 10:12PM Eastern, Team YouTube <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://x.com/TeamYouTube/status/2023958868372676770" data-i13n="cpos:5;pos:1">posted on X</a> that the issue has been completely fixed. While it didn’t say why YouTube went down, the team acknowledged the problem before 9PM and posted an update 20 minutes later that its recommendation system was having issues, even though its homepage was back. </p><p><strong>Update, February 17, 2026, 10:27 PM ET: </strong>YouTube says the issue has been completely fixed.</p><p><strong>Update, February 17, 2026, 10:08 PM ET:</strong> Updated with reports that certain Google services are also down for some users.</p><p><strong>Update, February 17, 2026, 9:34 PM ET:</strong> Updated with reports from users.</p><p><strong>Update, February 17, 2026, 9:26 PM ET:</strong> Updated to correct time of outage, added new countries where it’s out and added new reports of YouTube still being inaccessible.</p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/big-tech/youtube-was-down-for-thousands-of-users-in-the-us-020718788.html?src=rss
