The company is making the change as part of an effort to simplify its various product efforts, according to a memo cited by the <em>WSJ</em> from Fidji Simo, OpenAI's CEO of Applications. Fragmentation "has been slowing us down and making it harder to hit the quality bar we want," Simo said.</p>
