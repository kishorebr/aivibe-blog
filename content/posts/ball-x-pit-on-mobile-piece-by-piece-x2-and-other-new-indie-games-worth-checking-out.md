<p>Welcome to our latest roundup of what's going on in the indie game space. A bunch of intriguing games arrived this week, including a mobile port of one of the most absorbing things I’ve played in years and two completely different titles with the same name. Let’s get things started with a look at a few projects that were featured in the latest edition of the <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iJQzdmAWyR4" data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1">Future Games Show</a>.</p><div><div style="left:0;width:100%;height:0;position:relative;padding-bottom:56.25%;"><iframe src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/Zs-2lGGapeU?rel=0" style="top:0;left:0;width:100%;height:100%;position:absolute;border:0;" allowfullscreen scrolling="no" data-embed-domain="youtube.com" data-provider-name="youtube"></iframe></div></div><p><em>Hyperwired </em>(from SidralGames and publisher SelectaPlay) is a 2D roguelike shooter with an interesting resource-management twist. To recharge your weapons and systems, you have to plug a cable that trails behind your spaceship into a socket. While you're plugged in, your movement is restricted by the length of the tether, but you gain more firepower.&nbsp;</p><p>There are a whole bunch of upgrades and bullet modifiers to play around with here, including a slow-motion system you can activate at almost any time. <em>Hyperwired </em>is slated to hit <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://store.steampowered.com/app/3234850/HYPERWIRED/" data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1">Steam</a>, PS4, PS5, Xbox One, Xbox Series X/S and Nintendo Switch this summer.</p><div><div style="left:0;width:100%;height:0;position:relative;padding-bottom:56.25%;"><iframe src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/GASh-UJoGY0?rel=0" style="top:0;left:0;width:100%;height:100%;position:absolute;border:0;" allowfullscreen scrolling="no" data-embed-domain="youtube.com" data-provider-name="youtube"></iframe></div></div><p>In <em>Clean Up Earth,</em> you and other players can work together to restore polluted environments. You can play solo if you like, but on the larger maps you'll need to team up with others to handle large bits of junk. One particularly neat aspect of <em>Clean Up Earth</em> is that in-game actions will automatically trigger micro-donations from developer Magic Pockets and its partners to environmental organizations.</p><p><em>Clean Up Earth</em> is coming to <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://store.steampowered.com/app/3402720/Clean_Up_Earth/" data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1">Steam</a>, Epic Games Store, PS5, and Xbox Series X/S on April 2. A Nintendo Switch 2 version is on the way in the future. As with some <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/gaming/cloverpit-a-balatro-style-game-with-a-grungy-slot-machine-hits-ios-and-android-on-december-17-154500028.html" data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1">other roguelike deckbuilders</a>, you're trapped in a creepy situation and the only way to escape alive is to gamble and earn enough money in time to meet quotas. To do that, you'll need to twist the odds in your favor by building multipliers and synergies. You can boost your deck with powerful cards you can buy from a shop.</p><p>However, there are dangerous JERRY cards on the board that could spell doom if you flip them over. You can use hints and strategies to try to figure out where those cards are and avoid them.&nbsp;</p><p>There's no release date as yet for <em>Mr. Magpie’s Harmless Card Game</em>, which is from Giant Light Studios. However, you can request access to a playtest on <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://store.steampowered.com/app/3616280/Mr_Magpies_Harmless_Card_Game/" data-i13n="cpos:5;pos:1">Steam</a>.</p><div><div style="left:0;width:100%;height:0;position:relative;padding-bottom:56.25%;"><iframe src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/XLrH0jS695g?rel=0" style="top:0;left:0;width:100%;height:100%;position:absolute;border:0;" allowfullscreen scrolling="no" data-embed-domain="youtube.com" data-provider-name="youtube"></iframe></div></div><p>A press release described <em>Herdles</em> as "Spyro meets <em>Breath of the Wild</em>, with a dog." I'm immediately sold. </p><p>Playing as a magical version of creative director Christian Hübel’s own dog, Snoopy, you'll "restore balance to a fracturing world" in this open-world platformer. On your journey, you'll rescue Herdles, or corrupted creatures. Doing so will unlock new powers, such as being able to glide, bust through walls and swim up waterfalls.</p><p>There's no combat or death in this game, which seems to be largely about solving puzzles, experimenting with physics-based abilities and exploring. It's said to have "deep accessibility and customization options" too. You'll need to drag these items back to your base so you can upgrade your character, ship, shield and weapon. There are relics to find and you can swap these for installations like lasers, barriers and cannons. There are also puzzle-based sections that sees your character leave their ship for some in-person mining and upgrade collecting, temporarily switching from 2D to 3D action.</p><p>After a while, the monolith starts to thrum with an ominous sound. It looks cute! </p><p>You can decorate your shop and make it homely by cleaning, keeping the log fire burning and making sure the cookie jar is full. Of course, you'll be fixing up heirlooms and antiques for customers too. It's out now on <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://store.steampowered.com/app/3179050/Piece_by_Piece/" data-i13n="cpos:13;pos:1">Steam</a> for $12, with a 20 percent discount until March 25.</p><div><div style="left:0;width:100%;height:0;position:relative;padding-bottom:56.25%;"><iframe src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/xpO2mpcW9gk?rel=0" style="top:0;left:0;width:100%;height:100%;position:absolute;border:0;" allowfullscreen scrolling="no" data-embed-domain="youtube.com" data-provider-name="youtube"></iframe></div></div><p><em>Piece by Piece </em>is a puzzle platformer in the most literal sense. You manipulate levels by moving puzzle pieces around. It's a great idea from Neon Polygons and I'm keen to check this one out on <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://store.steampowered.com/app/3249380/Piece_by_Piece/" data-i13n="cpos:14;pos:1">Steam</a>. It typically costs $13, but there's a 15 percent discount until March 27.</p><p>Wait a second here... Two games called <em>Piece by Piece</em> that were released in the same week? This action adventure sees you on a mission to destroy every piece of machinery inside a ruined megastructure.</p><p><em>MotorSlice </em>is coming to <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://store.steampowered.com/app/2830030/MOTORSLICE/" data-i13n="cpos:20;pos:1">Steam</a> this spring.
