However, all of your companion’s other components are built into its abode, including dual far-field mics so you can talk to it, a down-firing full-range speaker so it can talk and an HD camera with Kira looks like a TikTok e-girl decked out in a frilly outfit complete with Razer neon green accents, while Zane is her edgy masculine alternative who kind of reminds me of the <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://en.meming.world/wiki/Giga_Chad" data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1">Giga Chad meme</a>, but with extra snake tattoos. Then there’s Sao, who appears to be directly inspired by iconic <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://knowyourmeme.com/memes/japanese-salarywoman-saori-araki" data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1">Japanese salary woman Saori Araki</a>. Finally, there’s an avatar made in the likeness of Faker (Lee Sang-hyeok), the most successful and well-known League of Legends player of all time and one of Razer&#39;s sponsored esports athletes.</p><figure><img src="https://d29szjachogqwa.cloudfront.net/images/user-uploaded/project-ava-connected.jpg" data-crop-orig-src="https://d29szjachogqwa.cloudfront.net/images/user-uploaded/project-ava-connected.jpg" style="height:1200px;width:1920px;" alt="The new peripheral for Project Ava is a cylinder that can display a 5.5-inch hologram of an AI companion." data-uuid="b033651e-b4c3-4f15-970d-0ef496c4dd1e"><figcaption>The new peripheral for Project Ava is a cylinder that can display a 5.5-inch hologram of an AI companion.</figcaption><div class="photo-credit">Sam Rutherford for Engadget</div></figure><p>The idea now is that instead of being trapped inside your computer, Ava or one of Razer’s other personas can sit on your desk and be your companion for everything. They can remind you of upcoming events, respond to questions or even comment on your outfit using Razer’s built-in camera. That said, if you need some privacy, the device’s mics can be muted and the company says its planning on putting a physical camera shutter on final retail models. Of course, Ava or any of the other avatars can still hang out while you game and give you advice. During my demo, Kira helped pick out a loadout in <em>Battlefield 6</em>  based on user criteria and even provided pros and cons for some of the game’s other equipment options.&nbsp;</p><figure><img src="https://d29szjachogqwa.cloudfront.net/images/user-uploaded/avatars.png" data-crop-orig-src="https://d29szjachogqwa.cloudfront.net/images/user-uploaded/avatars.png" style="height:633px;width:1507px;" alt="Project Ava&#39;s expanded roster of AI companions" data-uuid="b918f479-ae59-431a-8ce7-833d6d12618b"><figcaption>Project Ava&#39;s expanded roster of AI companions</figcaption><div class="photo-credit">Razer</div></figure><p>Unfortunately, while I did get to see Kira and Zane talk, dance and sway in their little bottles, Sao and Faker weren’t quite ready to make their holographic debuts. But according to Razer, that’s sort of by design as Project Ava is very much a work in progress. Currently, the avatars’ responses are generated by X AI’s Grok (<a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/ai/elon-musks-grok-ai-posted-csam-image-following-safeguard-lapses-140521454.html" data-i13n="cpos:5;pos:1">yikes!</a>), but the platform was created as a sort of open-source project that will support other models like Gemini or ChatGPT. </p><p>Down the line, Razer is hoping to add the ability for users to create their own unique avatars and companions based on their input or inspiration from real-world objects. Meanwhile, for avatars like Faker&#39;s because he’s also an actual person, Razer wants additional time to make the AI companion helpful with topics like real-time League of Legends coaching.</p><figure><img src="https://d29szjachogqwa.cloudfront.net/images/user-uploaded/project-ava-zane.jpg" data-crop-orig-src="https://d29szjachogqwa.cloudfront.net/images/user-uploaded/project-ava-zane.jpg" style="height:1200px;width:1920px;" alt="Say hello to Giga Chad, I mean Zane. " data-uuid="7ffdfb19-2c7b-42de-8512-058812f7f629"><figcaption>Say hello to Giga Chad, I mean Zane. </figcaption><div class="photo-credit">Sam Rutherford for Engadget</div></figure><p>That said, while some folks might find Project Ava a bit weird or unnerving, it actually feels pretty tame (almost cute even) in an era where people are already <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.theguardian.com/tv-and-radio/2025/jul/12/i-felt-pure-unconditional-love-the-people-who-marry-their-ai-chatbots" data-i13n="cpos:6;pos:1">marrying their AI partners</a>. And if you’re the kind of person who prefers digital companions over flesh-and-blood alternatives (you know, people), I guess it’s kind of nice to have a more tangible representation of your electronic waifus and husbandos.</p><figure><img src="https://d29szjachogqwa.cloudfront.net/images/user-uploaded/faker.jpg" data-crop-orig-src="https://d29szjachogqwa.cloudfront.net/images/user-uploaded/faker.jpg" style="height:1200px;width:1920px;" alt="Faker&#39;s avatar was only viewable in this nearly life-size mock up. " data-uuid="859ecd70-9b56-4e9f-9d27-ec459d4d17aa"><figcaption>Faker&#39;s avatar was only viewable in this nearly life-size mock up. </figcaption><div class="photo-credit">Sam Rutherford for Engadget</div></figure><p>Sadly, Razer has not provided full pricing for Project Ava’s holographic peripheral, though a representative said that it will be in the same ballpark as the company’s other peripherals.
