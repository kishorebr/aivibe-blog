<h2 id="jump-link-what-to-expect">What to expect</h2>
<p>Here's what LG has already confirmed it will be showcasing at CES 2026:&nbsp;</p>
<ul>
<li><p>LG will debut its first <a data-i13n="cpos:6;pos:1" href="https://www.engadget.com/home/home-theater/lg-will-debut-its-first-micro-rgb-television-at-ces-010037923.html">Micro RGB TV</a>, a display with a cutting-edge screen technology with multicolor backlights that should one-up mini LED displays. The size options are 100 inches, 86 inches and 75 inches.</p></li>
<li><p>The company is countering Samsung's Frame TVs with its new <a data-i13n="cpos:7;pos:1" href="https://www.engadget.com/home/home-theater/lg-to-unveil-a-canvas-style-tv-at-ces-2026-010024691.html">LG Gallery TV</a>, arriving in 55- and 65-inch screen sizes.</p></li>
<li><p>Look for a new LG humanoid <a data-i13n="cpos:8;pos:1" href="https://www.engadget.com/home/smart-home/lg-will-show-off-a-humanoid-robot-for-household-chores-at-ces-2026-145411218.html">home automation robot named CLOiD</a> to take the stage.&nbsp;</p></li>
<li><p>In the audio realm, the Korean multinational will also introduce a <a data-i13n="cpos:9;pos:1" href="https://www.engadget.com/audio/speakers/dolby-and-lg-introduce-a-modular-home-audio-system-for-ces-2026-010000126.html">Dolby-powered modular home audio system</a> and a new line of its <a data-i13n="cpos:10;pos:1" href="https://www.engadget.com/audio/speakers/lg-announces-new-line-of-xboom-speakers-ahead-of-ces-010052598.html">xboom speakers</a> (developed with musician will.i.am).&nbsp;</p></li>
</ul>
<p>Does that leave any surprises for the CES press conference? We'll find out on January 5.&nbsp;</p>
