<p>At GTC 2026, NVIDIA and Bolt announced what they hope will be a symbiotic partnership. Bolt gets NVIDIA technology that would be costly and impractical to build on its own. Meanwhile, NVIDIA not only gains a major customer but also access to the European rideshare company’s driving data.</p><p>Bolt says its fleet data will build a "learning engine" for autonomous vehicles (AVs) using NVIDIA tech. The rideshare company will use <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/ai/nvidia-ceo-jensen-huang-welcomes-the-rise-of-superintelligent-ai-at-ces-2025-002827074.html" data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1">NVIDIA Cosmos</a> to curate and search driving data. It will tap into <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/nvidia-ai-voice-avatar-omniverse-project-tokkio-142456487.html/1000" data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1">NVIDIA Omniverse</a> to reconstruct digital twins of real-world driving logs, then use Cosmos again to generate and augment data at scale.</p><p>NVIDIA's <a target="_blank" class="link rapid-with-clickid" href="https://shopping.yahoo.com/rdlw?merchantId=f52348f6-2304-415e-b16c-1fb252794066&amp;siteId=us-engadget&amp;pageId=1p-autolink&amp;contentUuid=0d967013-f22e-4eab-8b8e-5bd9867a0bc0&amp;featureId=text-link&amp;merchantName=NVIDIA&amp;linkText=Alpamayo+model&amp;custData=eyJzb3VyY2VOYW1lIjoiV2ViLURlc2t0b3AtVmVyaXpvbiIsImxhbmRpbmdVcmwiOiJodHRwczovL3d3dy5udmlkaWEuY29tL2VuLXVzL3NvbHV0aW9ucy9hdXRvbm9tb3VzLXZlaGljbGVzL2FscGFtYXlvLyIsImNvbnRlbnRVdWlkIjoiMGQ5NjcwMTMtZjIyZS00ZWFiLThiOGUtNWJkOTg2N2EwYmMwIiwib3JpZ2luYWxVcmwiOiJodHRwczovL3d3dy5udmlkaWEuY29tL2VuLXVzL3NvbHV0aW9ucy9hdXRvbm9tb3VzLXZlaGljbGVzL2FscGFtYXlvLyJ9&amp;signature=AQAAATU2HnF4qlOVFztE6RygEO-fQe2WboQJ8X3NmwuqEzvz&amp;gcReferrer=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.nvidia.com%2Fen-us%2Fsolutions%2Fautonomous-vehicles%2Falpamayo%2F" data-i13n="elm:affiliate_link;sellerN:NVIDIA;elmt:;cpos:3;pos:1" data-original-link="https://www.nvidia.com/en-us/solutions/autonomous-vehicles/alpamayo/">Alpamayo model</a>, designed specifically for AVs, will help the AI learn how to drive safely and appropriately in European cities. Finally, Bolt will integrate <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/ai/everything-nvidia-ceo-jensen-huang-announced-at-its-ces-2025-keynote-174947827.html" data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1">NVIDIA's Drive Hyperion platform</a> into its AVs.</p><p>"Autonomous vehicles require a full-stack approach that unifies AI models, high-performance compute, and a robust sensor architecture," NVIDIA EMEA Automotive VP Philippe Van Den Berge said. "By combining Bolt's real-world operational data with the NVIDIA Drive Hyperion platform, AI infrastructure, and open models &amp; libraries across Omniverse, Cosmos, and Alpamayo, we're enabling a scalable foundation for safe, high-performance autonomous mobility services designed for the complexity and diversity of European roads."</p><p>Bolt has been busy gearing up for an autonomous future.
