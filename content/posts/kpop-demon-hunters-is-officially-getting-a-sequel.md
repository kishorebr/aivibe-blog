<p><em>KPop Demon Hunters</em> is getting a sequel, <a target="_blank" class="no-affiliate-link link" href="https://www.netflix.com/tudum/articles/kpop-demon-hunters-sequel" data-i13n="elm:context_link;elmt:doNotAffiliate;cpos:1;pos:1">Netflix and Sony have announced</a>. Sony Pictures Animation handed the rights to the film to Netflix <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/sony-netflix-exclusive-deal-182221930.html" data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1">in 2021</a> as part of a larger licensing deal, but neither company could have expected how much of a hit it would ultimately become. Besides being Netflix's <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/entertainment/streaming/kpop-demon-hunters-is-netflixs-most-watched-movie-of-all-time-215857627.html" data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1">"most-watched movie of all time,"</a> <em>KPop Demon Hunters</em> is also nominated for Best Animated Feature and Best Original Song at <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/entertainment/tv-movies/netflix-scores-oscar-nominations-for-frankenstein-and-kpop-demon-hunters-163521141.html" data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1">the 98th Academy Awards</a>, and stands a good chance of winning.</p><p>Maggie Kang and Chris Appelhans, the directors of the first film, are returning to direct the sequel. The project will be the first in the duo's new "exclusive multiyear writing and directing partnership" with Netflix, which is focused on animation. "I feel immense pride as a Korean filmmaker that the audience wants more from this Korean story and our Korean characters," Kang said in a statement. "There's so much more to this world we have built, and I'm excited to show you. This is only the beginning."</p><div><blockquote class="twitter-tweet" style="width:640px;height:684px;width:640px;height:684px;"><p lang="en" dir="ltr">IT'S OFFICIAL HUNTERS 💫&nbsp; KPOP DEMON HUNTERS will return for a sequel written and directed by Maggie Kang and Chris Appelhans. <br><br>“I feel immense pride as a Korean filmmaker that the audience wants more from this Korean story and our Korean characters. </div><p>"These characters are like family to us, their world has become our second home," Appelhans said. "We're excited to write their next chapter, challenge them, and watch them evolve — and continue pushing the boundaries of how music, animation, and story can come together."</p><p>To put <em>KPop Demon Hunters</em> popularity into perspective, the film had such a wide reach that Netflix was willing to set aside its aversion to theatrical releases and put it in theaters after it premiered on streaming. <em>KPop Demon Hunters</em> <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://deadline.com/2025/08/box-office-kpop-demon-hunters-weapons-1236495263/" data-i13n="cpos:5;pos:1">reportedly made over $19 million</a> during its <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/entertainment/streaming/netflixs-kpop-demon-hunters-is-headed-to-theaters-for-two-days-124552307.html" data-i13n="cpos:6;pos:1">initial two-day theatrical run</a> in August 2025, and Netflix has brought it back to theaters multiple times since then. That's on top of the more than 500 million views the film racked up on Netflix itself. Not making a sequel would essentially be leaving money on the table.</p><p><a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://puck.news/why-did-kpop-demon-hunters-go-straight-to-streaming/" data-i13n="cpos:7;pos:1">According to <em>Puck</em></a>, the structure of Netflix's deal with Sony means it will likely be the only company directly profiting off a <em>KPop Demon Hunters</em> follow-up, however. "While Sony has the contractual right to produce any sequels or spinoffs," <em>Puck</em> reports, "it will make no additional money from the runaway success of the first film." Sweetening that deal could be one reason Netflix and Sony Pictures <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/entertainment/streaming/netflixs-expanded-sony-deal-includes-streaming-rights-to-the-legend-of-zelda-movie-203011384.html" data-i13n="cpos:8;pos:1">expanded their film licensing partnership</a> in January, a deal that reportedly cost the streaming service <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://variety.com/2026/film/news/sony-netflix-streaming-movies-1236632330/" data-i13n="cpos:9;pos:1">over $7 billion</a> to secure.</p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/entertainment/streaming/kpop-demon-hunters-is-officially-getting-a-sequel-195038954.html?src=rss
