<p>In the mad dash many companies have made to incorporate AI features into their phones, Nothing arrived at one of the better ideas with <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/mobile/smartphones/nothing-phone-3a-essential-spaces-ai-powered-app-done-right-163042976.html" data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1">Essential Space</a> on the <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/mobile/smartphones/nothing-phone-3a-and-3a-pro-review-rising-above-the-boring-competition-120014496.html" data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1">Nothing Phone 3a</a> in 2025. The AI-powered app turns screenshots and voice recordings into actionable to-do lists and transcriptions, and now Nothing is <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://nothing.community/d/54241-essential-space-major-update" data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1">rolling out an update</a> to make the app easier to search and capable of recognizing new kinds of content.</p><p>As part of the update, Essential Space now recognizes &quot;Events,&quot; displaying them in their own card with fields for the date, time and location. That means, for example, if you add a photo of a flyer for pottery class to the app, Essential Space will be able to pull the details of when and where it&#39;s happening, and track it in much the same way it does tasks or to-dos. Nothing foresees events being such a big part of how people will use Essential Space that it&#39;s also changing the layout of the app&#39;s interface and listing things like Events and Tasks in a new For You page you see when you open the app.</p><p>To make everything you&#39;ve stored in Essential Space easier to find, the app now also supports semantic search, surfacing results that don&#39;t just match the text you&#39;ve entered, but try to match the meaning of what you&#39;re looking for. Semantic search should be particularly useful when you&#39;re looking for an image, because you can enter a description of what you&#39;re looking for and Essential Space should still be able to surface it.</p><p>Sorting and indexing digital ephemera like voice notes and screenshots with AI is a popular use for the technology. Google offers <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/apps/the-pixel-screenshots-app-uses-ai-to-scour-the-screengrabs-i-cant-remember-why-i-saved-170043423.html" data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1">Pixel Screenshots</a>, and even Apple gave iOS and iPadOS the ability to <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/ai/ios-26-screenshots-could-be-an-intriguing-preview-of-apples-delayed-siri-rework-183005404.html" data-i13n="cpos:5;pos:1">automatically recognize events in images</a> and add them to your calendar. Essential Space might be less unique now, but the fact that Nothing continues to update it bodes well for its future.</p><p>Nothing&#39;s new Essential Space update is available starting today on &quot;all 2025–2026 Nothing and CMF phones that support Essential Key,&quot; the company says. Essential Space should automatically update, but you also manually update the app in the Google Play Store.</p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/apps/nothing-updates-its-ai-app-with-semantic-search-and-a-new-way-to-track-events-202500495.html?src=rss
