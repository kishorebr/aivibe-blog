<p>A brand spanking-new FoloToy teddy bear can be yours once again. However, he may now be less knowledgeable about spanking. The infamous &quot;Kumma&quot; children&#39;s AI teddy bear, once <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/ai/sales-of-a-teddy-bear-were-suspended-because-of-its-sexually-explicit-ai-233127354.html" data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1">an expert in BDSM and knife-fetching</a>, is back on sale. The company claims the toy now has stronger child safety protections in place.</p><p>The Singapore-based FoloToy suspended sales of Kumma last week after a research group published an eyebrow-raising report. The PIRG Education Fund found that the fuzzy little teddy had a few spicy secrets.</p><p>The review discovered that the AI toy had a thing for blades and kinky bedroom play. The bear had no problem suggesting where to find knives in the home. And it not only replied to sexual prompts but also expanded on them. Researchers say it ran with their explicit cues, escalating them in graphic detail and &quot;introducing new sexual concepts of its own.&quot; It explained sex positions, gave step-by-step instructions for sexual bondage and detailed various role-playing scenarios.
