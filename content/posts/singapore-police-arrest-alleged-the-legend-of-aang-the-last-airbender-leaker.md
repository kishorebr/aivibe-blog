<p>People aren&#39;t thrilled with Paramount these days. After all, <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/entertainment/teamsters-urge-doj-to-block-paramounts-warner-bros-merger-215115721.html" data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1">corporate consolidation</a> and the <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.thedailybeast.com/donald-trump-approved-cbs-boss-bari-weiss-going-full-propaganda-palooza-over-iran-war/" data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1">transformation</a> of CBS News into state media tend to do that. But here&#39;s someone who may not have chosen the… wisest form of protest. <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://gizmodo.com/avatar-movie-leaks-arrest-paramount-2000750466" data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1"><em>The Straits Times</em></a> reports (via <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://gizmodo.com/avatar-movie-leaks-arrest-paramount-2000750466" data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1"><em>Gizmodo</em></a>) that Singapore police have arrested a 26-year-old man for leaking Paramount&#39;s <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/entertainment/tv-movies/the-legend-of-aang-the-last-airbender-gets-hit-with-its-second-delay-175913128.html" data-i13n="cpos:5;pos:1"><em>The Legend of Aang: The Last Airbender</em></a>.</p><p>The film, part of the <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/entertainment/tv-movies/theres-a-new-avatar-the-last-airbender-cartoon-coming-to-nickelodeon-182956881.html" data-i13n="cpos:6;pos:1"><em>Avatar: The Last Airbender </em>franchise</a>, was initially scheduled for a theatrical release. But after two delays, it was later moved to Paramount+, where it’s set to debut later this year. Naturally, hardcore fans of the franchise bristled at the prospect of the highly anticipated entry skipping theaters. Paramount&#39;s scant marketing apparently didn&#39;t help either.</p><p>However, many have already seen the movie, allegedly thanks to the unnamed 26-year-old in question. Singapore police said he gained remote access to a server and downloaded the film. He then posted parts of it online. After the leak, Paramount changed the movie&#39;s name to <em>Avatar Aang: The Last Airbender</em> — perhaps to try to prevent casual fans&#39; searches from turning up info about the leak.</p><p>The man is being investigated for unauthorized access to computer material. He could face a maximum of seven years in prison, a fine of up to $50,000, or both.</p><p>Earlier this month, <em>The Hollywood Reporter</em> <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/business/digital/behind-the-hacker-leak-legend-aang-the-last-airbender-1236566054/" data-i13n="cpos:7;pos:1">interviewed</a> the X account holder @ImStillDissin, who posted the clips. (Although we can speculate that this may be the man currently in custody, that’s unconfirmed.) The interview revealed a rather, shall we say, blasé approach to the incident. He said he figured posting clips from the movie was no biggie since the film is a streaming-only release. &quot;I saw it&#39;s just a Paramount+ thing, so I decided I&#39;d troll a little bit,&quot; the leaker said.</p><p>The leaked clips spread rapidly. Despite pleas from 4Chan posters to share the entire film, @ImStillDissin resisted. However, someone else shared the full movie by April 13. Naturally, that file has since circulated far and wide.</p><p>So, good luck with that official October 9 streaming release, Paramount.
