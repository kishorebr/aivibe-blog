<p>The big reveal for Meta&#39;s next mixed reality glasses is being postponed until the first half of 2027, according to a report from <a data-i13n="elm:context_link;elmt:doNotAffiliate;cpos:1;pos:1" class="no-affiliate-link" href="https://www.businessinsider.com/meta-delays-new-mixed-reality-glasses-code-named-phoenix-2025-12"><em>Business Insider</em></a>. Based on an internal memo from Maher Saba, the vice president of Meta&#39;s Reality Labs Foundation, the report said that the company&#39;s project, which is codenamed &quot;Phoenix,&quot; will no longer be scheduled for a 2026 debut.</p>
<p>In a separate memo, Meta execs explained that the delay would help deliver a more &quot;polished and reliable experience.&quot; According to <em>BI</em>, a memo from Meta&#39;s Gabriel Aul and Ryan Cairns said this new release window is &quot;going to give us a lot more breathing room to get this right.&quot; Meta hasn&#39;t publicly revealed many details about its Phoenix project, but <a data-i13n="elm:context_link;elmt:doNotAffiliate;cpos:2;pos:1" class="no-affiliate-link" href="https://www.theinformation.com/articles/bytedance-developing-mixed-reality-goggles-challenge-meta"><em>The Information</em></a> previously reported that it would feature a goggle-like form factor with an external power source, similar to how the <a data-i13n="elm:context_link;elmt:doNotAffiliate;cpos:3;pos:1" class="no-affiliate-link" href="https://www.engadget.com/ar-vr/apple-vision-pro-m5-review-a-better-beta-is-still-a-beta-130000284.html">Apple Vision Pro</a> is attached to a battery pack.</p>
<span id="end-legacy-contents"></span><p>In the memo from Saba, <em>BI</em> reported that Meta is also working on a &quot;limited edition&quot; wearable with the codename &quot;Malibu 2.&quot; Yesterday, Meta announced its acquisition of <a data-i13n="elm:context_link;elmt:doNotAffiliate;cpos:4;pos:1" class="no-affiliate-link" href="https://www.engadget.com/ai/metas-latest-acquisition-suggests-hardware-plans-beyond-glasses-and-headsets-212930339.html">Limitless</a>, a startup that recently developed an AI wearable called Pendant. Even though Meta&#39;s current product portfolio is dominated by smart glasses and <a data-i13n="elm:context_link;elmt:doNotAffiliate;cpos:5;pos:1" class="no-affiliate-link" href="https://www.engadget.com/meta-quest-3-review-mixed-reality-vr-150009788.html">VR headsets</a>, the Limitless acquisition and Malibu 2 project could hint at the company&#39;s plans to expand its offerings.</p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/ar-vr/meta-plans-to-push-back-the-debut-of-its-next-mixed-reality-glasses-to-2027-172437374.html?src=rss
