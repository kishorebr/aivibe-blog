<p>After debuting in the US, Gemini in Chrome is making its way to more markets. Starting today, Google is rolling out Chrome&#39;s built-in chatbot to users in Asia and the Pacific, including Australia, Indonesia, Japan, the Philippines, Singapore, South Korea and Vietnam. The expansion comes after Google earlier this year made Gemini in Chrome available to people in <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/ai/google-starts-rolling-out-gemini-in-chrome-to-users-in-canada-india-and-new-zealand-023000528.html" data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1">Canada, India and New Zealand</a>.&nbsp;</p><p>With the exception of Japan, where Google isn&#39;t making the new suite available on iOS just yet, everyone else in the countries mentioned above can access Gemini in Chrome through Chrome&#39;s desktop browser, and the app on their iPhone or iPad. To get started, just tap the &quot;Ask Gemini&quot; icon at the top right of the screen. It will open a new sidebar Google introduced at the start of the year where you can chat with Gemini across every open tab. From there, you can also access Google&#39;s in-house image generator, <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/ai/googles-nano-banana-pro-image-generator-leverages-gemini-3-for-improved-visuals-and-text-rendering-185505073.html" data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1">Nano Banana 2</a>. As you would expect, the suite offers integrations with Google&#39;s other apps, allowing you, for instance, to add events to Calendar without leaving the interface.&nbsp;</p><p>If you don&#39;t want to use Gemini, you can right click on the shortcut to unpin it from the top of the interface. &nbsp; &nbsp;</p><p><strong>Update 7:43PM ET</strong>: This article has been updated to reflect the expansion includes the entire Asia-Pacific region. </p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/ai/google-brings-gemini-in-chrome-to-users-in-asia-and-the-pacific-220000698.html?src=rss
