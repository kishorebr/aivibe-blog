<p>Sony just revealed a trio of <a data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1" href="https://blog.playstation.com/2026/04/01/playstation-plus-monthly-games-for-april-lords-of-the-fallen-tomb-raider-i-iii-remastered-sword-art-online-fractured-daydream/"><ins>PlayStation Plus Monthly Games for April</ins></a> and it&#39;s a pretty stacked lineup. These will all be playable on April 7 for subscribers on any tier. After downloading, the games will stay in a player&#39;s library as long as the subscription remains active.</p>
<div id="2a36fe5efde24dddbad028db7706619f"><iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/yI9ID7BLqqw?si=Lu_4F0qhXmKcK_o-" title="YouTube video player" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></div>
<p>First up, there&#39;s <em>Lords of the Fallen </em>for PS5. This is a sequel to <a data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1" href="https://www.engadget.com/2014-07-09-lords-of-the-fallen-challenges-you-to-pick-it-up-in-october.html"><ins>2014&#39;s </ins><em><ins>Lords of the Fallen</ins></em></a>, despite having the same exact name. The 2023 release boasts a much larger world than the original, but similar fast-paced gameplay. It&#39;s an action RPG with nine character classes and hundreds of weapons to choose from. There&#39;s also a dual-world mechanic that&#39;s (sort of) like <em>The Legend of Zelda: A Link to the Past</em>. It was generally well-reviewed and a success with players. There&#39;s another sequel scheduled for release later this year.</p>
<span id="end-legacy-contents"></span><div id="4cebf7f8f9f846839b392706108615fd"><iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/Q_vH_0gpVlk?si=77vcWVjJO3cNHl_s" title="YouTube video player" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></div>
<p><em>Tomb Raider I-III Remastered</em> is a collection of ports first released back in 2024. These updated versions of old-school PlayStation classics boast updated graphics, with the ability to instantly switch back to the retro polygonal look. There&#39;s a new challenge mode that offers players the ability to replay levels with customized modifiers to complete achievements. This is a great entry point for Lara Croft fans who never got to play the originals. The collection is available on PS4 and PS5.</p>
<div id="6ebc550e4a8e471cae6d62bab74d8673"><iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/Lz275WkdChI?si=u7YfOlgucalph5WS" title="YouTube video player" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></div>
<p><em>Sword Art Online Fractured Daydream</em> is a live-service action RPG for PS5 owners with a heavy emphasis on co-op gameplay. Around 20 players can adventure simultaneously in teams of five parties scattered throughout the world. The story is a bit of a mess, pulling in characters from across the franchise&#39;s many entries. This approach sort of reminds me of something like <em>Persona Q: Shadow of the Labyrinth</em>, which uses narrative trickery to allow characters from multiple games to team up.</p>
<p>As always, the introduction of new titles means that some old games have gotta go.
