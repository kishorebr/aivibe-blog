<p>Dyson just announced its first-ever handheld fan, the HushJet Mini Cool. As the name suggests, it uses the company&#39;s proprietary HushJet air projection system. This tech first showed up on an air purifier that <a data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1" href="https://www.engadget.com/home/smart-home/best-air-purifier-120040002.html"><ins>we found to be exceptionally quiet</ins></a>.</p>
<p>Dyson promises the fan can deliver focused airflow of up to 25m/s, which works out to 55mph. The brushless motor spins up to 65,000 RPM. This thing looks like a legitimate cooling system, despite its size. It also weighs just 7.5 ounces.&nbsp;</p>
<span id="end-legacy-contents"></span><p>It offers five speeds and a boost mode, which should be useful during that next heat wave. It charges via USB-C and ships with a charging stand. The fan can also stand on its own, making it a decent choice for a desk. The rechargeable battery can get up to six hours of use per charge.</p>

<p>The HushJet Mini Cool costs $100, which is cheap for a Dyson product but expensive for a handheld fan. It&#39;s available in a trio of colorways. The gray model is available tomorrow.
