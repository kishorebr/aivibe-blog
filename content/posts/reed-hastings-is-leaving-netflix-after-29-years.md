<p>Netflix co-founder and current chairman Reed Hastings is leaving the streaming company’s board in June to focus on &quot;his philanthropy and other pursuits,&quot; according to <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://s22.q4cdn.com/959853165/files/doc_financials/2026/q1/FINAL-Q1-26-Shareholder-Letter.pdf" data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1">a shareholder letter</a> released alongside Netflix&#39;s Q1 earnings. Hastings has served as chairman of Netflix&#39;s board since 2023, a role he assumed <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/netflix-reed-hastings-steps-down-ceo-211808104.html" data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1">after stepping down as co-CEO</a> and promoting Greg Peters in his place.</p><p>&quot;Netflix changed my life in so many ways, and my all‑time favorite memory was January 2016, when we enabled nearly the entire planet to enjoy our service,&quot; Hastings said in a statement. &quot;My real contribution at Netflix wasn’t a single decision; it was a focus on member joy, building a culture that others could inherit and improve, and building a company that could be both beloved by members and wildly successful for generations to come. A special thanks to Greg and Ted, whose commitment to Netflix’s greatness is so strong that I can now focus on new things.&quot;</p><p>Hastings founded Netflix in 1997 as a DVD-by-mail rental service with his co-founder and the company&#39;s first CEO Marc Randolph. In 1999, Hastings became CEO, and eventually led the company through its transformation into a streaming service in 2007. Netflix started producing its own television series and movies in 2013, and in 2020, the company&#39;s board <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2020-07-16/netflix-completes-hollywood-transformation-with-sarandos-s-move" data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1">named Ted Sarandos as Hasting&#39;s co-CEO</a>, in part to oversee its growing production business. Hastings stepped down as co-CEO in 2023 to become Netflix’s executive chairman, as then COO Greg Peters was promoted to co-CEO. Among his other contributions, Hasting is also the architect of Netflix&#39;s infamous <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://jobs.netflix.com/culture" data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1">&quot;culture memo,&quot;</a> which codified the company&#39;s high-performance culture.</p><p>While he&#39;ll no longer be on Netflix&#39;s board, Hastings still has a seat on the board of AI startup <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.anthropic.com/news/reed-hastings" data-i13n="cpos:5;pos:1">Anthropic</a> and media and financial software company <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.bloomberg.com/company/press/bloomberg-announces-new-board-of-directors/" data-i13n="cpos:6;pos:1">Bloomberg</a>. Netflix, for its part, is continuing to expand outside of the television and film business Hastings helped build, by offering <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/entertainment/streaming/netflix-is-bringing-party-games-to-tvs-123034128.html" data-i13n="cpos:7;pos:1">a selection of curated party games</a>, a growing <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/entertainment/streaming/netflix-will-air-new-video-podcasts-from-pete-davidson-and-michael-irvin-this-month-224353011.html" data-i13n="cpos:8;pos:1">library of video podcasts</a> and <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/entertainment/streaming/netflix-lands-a-deal-with-mlb-to-air-select-live-baseball-games-213336003.html" data-i13n="cpos:9;pos:1">live sports</a>.</p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/entertainment/streaming/reed-hastings-is-leaving-netflix-after-29-years-213136444.html?src=rss
