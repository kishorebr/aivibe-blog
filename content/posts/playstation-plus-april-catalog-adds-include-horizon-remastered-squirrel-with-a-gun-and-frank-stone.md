<p>For PlayStation Plus subscribers, April is going to be a little bit spooky, a tad sporty and extra squirrelly. PlayStation Plus Extra and Premium players <a data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1" href="https://blog.playstation.com/2026/04/15/playstation-plus-game-catalog-for-april-the-crew-motorfest-horizon-zero-dawn-remastered-football-manager-26-console-warriors-abyss-and-more/">will get access to</a> <em>The Crew Motorfest</em>, <em>Horizon Zero Dawn Remastered</em>, <em>Football Manager 26 Console</em>, <em>Warriors: Abyss</em>, <em>Squirrel with a Gun</em>, <em>The Casting of Frank Stone</em> and <em>Monster Train</em>. Additionally,<em>Wild Arms 4</em> will be exclusive to Premium libraries. Expect the full lineup to go live on April 21.</p>
<p><em>The Crew Motorfest</em>, <em>Horizon Zero Dawn Remastered</em>, <em>Warriors: Abyss </em>and <em>Wild Arms 4 </em>will hit PS4 and PS5 consoles, while the rest of the month&#39;s additions are PS5 only. In the case of <em>Horizon</em>, PS4 players will receive <em>Horizon Zero Dawn Complete Edition</em>, rather than the PS5 remaster.&nbsp;</p>
<span id="end-legacy-contents"></span><p>Horizon, The Crew and Football Manager are self-explanatory at this point in gaming history, but here&#39;s a quick rundown of the more underground titles on April&#39;s list: <em>Warriors: Abyss</em> is a hectic hack-and-slash roguelite from Koei Tecmo; <em>Squirrel with a Gun</em> is a silly yet competent third-person shooter from a two-man indie team; <em>Monster Train</em> is a much-loved demonic deckbuilder from Shiny Shoe and Good Shepherd Entertainment; and <em>Wild Arms 4</em> is a PS2-era RPG from Japanese studio Media.Vision.</p>

<p><em>The Casting of Frank Stone</em> is what PlayStation Plus was made for, in my estimation. It comes from Supermassive, a campy-horror studio that I&#39;m quite fond of, but it&#39;s a crossover with <em>Dead by Daylight</em>, a game I&#39;ve never played, despite a latent interest in its vibe. For whatever reason, <em>Frank Stone</em> never eclipsed other titles in my to-play pile and in the harsh light of 2026, I was on the verge of forgetting all about it. Now that it&#39;s being shoved in my digital face (<em>complimentary</em>), I&#39;m ready to give it a go. And who knows, maybe it&#39;ll be a gateway into the rich world of <em>Dead by Daylight</em>.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Most of the games on this month&#39;s list can fit this description to some degree — minus the <em>Dead by Daylight </em>hook, unless you really squint at <em>Monster Train</em> — so it feels like a quality batch.</p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/gaming/playstation/playstation-plus-april-catalog-adds-include-horizon-remastered-squirrel-with-a-gun-and-frank-stone-194534366.html?src=rss
