<p>Lucid Motors has recalled over 4,000 Gravity SUVs <a data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1" href="https://www.nhtsa.gov/?nhtsaId=26V192000"><ins>after discovering a problem with seat belts</ins></a>. The company told the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) that some of the anchors for the second-row seat belts weren&#39;t welded properly. This means that there&#39;s a chance they won&#39;t actually hold passengers during a collision.</p>
<p>The company blames the issue on a seat supplier changing its manufacturing process &quot;without notice to or approval by Lucid.&quot; It says it has reverted to the original design and that newer models aren&#39;t impacted by the recall. Vehicles manufactured after February 14, 2026 are good to go.</p>
<span id="end-legacy-contents"></span><p>Lucid will have to inspect each and every Gravity SUV affected by the recall and potentially install a new bracket or a whole new seat. Owners will receive instructions to bring their vehicles to service centers. The company hasn&#39;t reported any injuries related to the defect, which is good news.</p>
<p>4,000 may not sound like a particularly high number, but this isn&#39;t Toyota or Ford. The number accounts for nearly every Gravity SUV manufactured since it launched last year, <a data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1" href="https://www.techbuzz.ai/articles/lucid-recalls-4-000-gravity-suvs-over-seat-belt-defect"><ins>according to </ins><em><ins>The Tech Buzz</ins></em></a>. It&#39;s been reported that the company sold <a data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1" href="https://techcrunch.com/2026/01/05/lucid-motors-doubled-ev-output-in-2025-after-early-gravity-suv-struggles/"><ins>around 15,800 vehicles in 2025</ins></a>, but that accounts for every model in the lineup.</p>

<p>This isn&#39;t the first issue that has popped up regarding Lucid&#39;s Gravity SUV. The company had to recall many vehicles <a data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1" href="https://www.autoblog.com/news/lucids-new-gravity-suv-gets-its-first-recalllucids"><ins>due to an issue with the airbags</ins></a> at the tail-end of last year. The model has also been plagued by software issues, though numerous patches <a data-i13n="cpos:5;pos:1" href="https://www.motortrend.com/news/2026-lucid-gravity-electric-suv-bug-fix-software-update"><ins>seem to have cleared that up</ins></a>. The three-row electric SUV <a data-i13n="cpos:6;pos:1" href="https://www.engadget.com/transportation/evs/lucids-electric-gravity-suv-starts-at-79900-161020540.html"><ins>starts at $80,000</ins></a>.</p>
