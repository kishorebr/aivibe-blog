<p>A judge has <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://x.com/TheFIREorg/status/2045328581501325463?s=20" data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1">granted</a> the makers of the &quot;ICE Sightings - Chicagoland&quot; Facebook group and the Eyes Up app a preliminary injunction to stop the Trump administration from coercing platforms to take these projects down. Judge Jorge L. Alonso of the United States District Court for the Northern District of Illinois found that the plaintiffs, Kassandra Rosado and Kreisau Group, are likely to succeed in their case, which alleges that the government suppressed protected speech under the First Amendment by strong-arming Facebook and Apple into removing ICE monitoring efforts.&nbsp;</p><p> Both <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/apps/apple-removed-an-app-that-archives-videos-of-ice-arrests-212946540.html" data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1">Eyes Up</a> and ICE Sightings - Chicagoland use publicly available information to keep tabs on ICE activity. But after pressure from Trump officials, they were removed from Apple&#39;s App Store and Facebook, respectively. Similar apps including <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/apps/apple-removes-iceblock-from-the-app-store-after-trump-administrations-demand-031659651.html" data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1">ICEBlock</a> and <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/apps/google-is-also-removing-apps-used-to-report-sightings-of-ice-agents-192940612.html" data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1">Red Dot</a> were also <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/big-tech/its-time-for-apple-to-reinstate-iceblock-220802356.html" data-i13n="cpos:5;pos:1">taken down from the App Store</a> and Google Play. The lawsuit cites social media posts by former US Attorney General Pam Bondi and former Secretary of Homeland Security Kristi Noem that demanded and took credit for the removal of these apps. In a document filed on Friday, Alonso called these posts &quot;thinly veiled threats.&quot;</p><p>The Foundation for Individual Rights and Expression (FIRE), which is defending the plaintiffs, wrote in a post on X that it is &quot;extremely encouraged by this ruling.&quot; It continued, &quot;Even though it’s not the end of the case, it bodes well for the future of our legal fight to ensure that the First Amendment protects the right to discuss, record, and criticize what law enforcement does in public.&quot;</p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/apps/judge-sides-with-creators-of-banned-ice-trackers-who-allege-dhs-and-doj-violated-their-first-amendment-rights-191701801.html?src=rss
