    Is ChatGPT Plus still worth your $20? I compared it to the Free, Go, and Pro plans - here's my advice
     
    Let's look at all of ChatGPT's plans to see if a subscription is worth it - especially since the free tier has so much.
      Written by 
            Elyse Betters Picaro, Senior Contributing EditorSenior Contributing Editor  Jan. 27, 2026 at 7:00 p.m. PT                             Elyse Betters Picaro / ZDNETFollow ZDNET: Add us as a preferred source on Google.    ZDNET's key takeaways ChatGPT Go adds more access for $8 a month, without upgrading to Plus.Plus stays best for priority access and higher limits across top features.Pro is for power users who need the highest caps and GPT-5.2 Pro.It's been a few years since ChatGPT first appeared, and since then, the AI chatbot has continuously updated with new models, features, limitations, and plans. While there's a lot you can do for free, some tools and perks are reserved for paid tiers.OpenAI introduced the $20 monthly Plus plan to manage demand and guarantee access during busy periods. But now, it also has ChatGPT Go, a new low-cost plan for just $8 per month.   OpenAI has also said that ads are coming soon in the US for the Go and Free tiers as part of early testing, while paid plans like Plus and Pro will stay ad-free. Also: How to use ChatGPT: A beginner's guideIt's therefore a good time to re-evaluate if Plus is worth it. Some of you may even be new to generative AI altogether, are wondering whether to try ChatGPT, and are confused about all the plans. Let's break it down. I'll help you finally decide whether ChatGPT Free, ChatGPT Go, ChatGPT Plus, or even the $200-per-month Pro subscription is right for you.
