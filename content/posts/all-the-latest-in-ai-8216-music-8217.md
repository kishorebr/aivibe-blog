<p class="has-text-align-none">AI has touched every part of the music industry, from <a href="https://www.theverge.com/entertainment/902537/ai-music-dont-ask-dont-tell">sample sourcing</a> and <a href="https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/829964/country-music-ai">demo recording</a>, to serving up <a href="https://www.theverge.com/entertainment/874945/spotify-about-the-song-ai-liner-notes">digital liner notes</a> and <a href="https://www.theverge.com/report/902005/apple-ai-playlist-playground-bad-at-music">building playlists</a>. There are technical and legal challenges, fierce ethical debates, and fears that the slop will simply crush working musicians through sheer volume. Is it art or just an output? What exactly <em>is</em> &#8220;<a href="https://www.theverge.com/report/825141/sunos-ceo-ai-text-prompt-really-active-music-creation">really active</a>&#8220;? Whether it&#8217;s a new model or a new lawsuit, we&#8217;re covering it all to make sure you don&#8217;t miss any major developments. </p>
