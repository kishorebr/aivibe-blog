<p class="has-text-align-none">OpenAI is going to let users access Codex, its desktop AI tool that can write code and use apps on your computer, from the ChatGPT app on your phone. </p>
<p class="has-text-align-none">Following the surge in popularity for Anthropic's Claude Code, OpenAI has been working quickly to try and catch up, including by cutting back <a href="https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/895835/openai-cuts-back-on-side-quests">on "side quests,"</a> shutting down projects like <a href="https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/902368/openai-sora-dead-ai-video-generation-competition">the Sora video-generation tool</a>, and focusing on <a href="https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/911118/openai-memo-cro-ai-competition-anthropic">growing its enterprise business</a>. The company's push included the recently released major update for Codex that lets it <a href="https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/913034/openai-codex-updates-use-macos">operate apps on macOS</a> - a potentially major step as part of its ambitions to <a href="https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/897778/openai-chatgpt-codex-atlas-browser-superapp">make a desktop "superapp."</a></p>
