<p>Anthropic is bringing another paid feature to Claude&#39;s free tier. The next time you chat with Claude, you&#39;ll have the option to have it reference your previous conversation to inform its outputs. Anthropic first made its chatbot capable of remembering past interactions <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/ai/claude-can-now-reference-past-chats-if-you-want-it-to-211806343.html" data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1">last August</a>, before giving it the ability to compartmentalize memories <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/ai/claude-can-now-compartmentalize-as-part-of-a-major-memory-upgrade-170000194.html" data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1">in the fall</a>. Making memory a free feature is well-timed; earlier today Anthropic made it easier for users to <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/ai/anthropics-claude-can-now-absorb-your-past-conversations-with-other-ai-chatbots-153201656.html" data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1">import their past conversations</a> with a competing chatbot to Claude.&nbsp;If after enabling memory you decide to turn it off, you can either pause the feature, preserving Claude’s memories for use down the road, or completely delete them so they’re not saved on Anthropic’s servers.    </p><p>Claude is enjoying new-found popularity, having recently jumped to the <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/big-tech/anthropics-claude-grabs-top-spot-in-app-store-after-trumps-ban-193610130.html" data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1">number one spot</a> in the App Store&#39;s free app charts. This comes while Anthropic is engaged in a high-stakes contract dispute with the US government over AI safeguards. On Friday, US Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth labeled the company a &quot;supply chain risk&quot; after it refused to sign a contract that would allow the Pentagon to use Anthropic models for mass surveillance against Americans and in fully autonomous weapons. Following Hegseth&#39;s announcement, Anthropic vowed to challenge the designation. As of right now, we’re waiting to see how things play out, and what it might mean for Anthropic. </p><p></p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/ai/anthropic-brings-memory-to-claudes-free-plan-220729070.html?src=rss
