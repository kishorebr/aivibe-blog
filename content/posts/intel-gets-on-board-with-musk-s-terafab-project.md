<p>Intel <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://x.com/intel/status/2041501301318766866?s=20" data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1">has announced</a> that it will help Elon Musk design and build his proposed <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://terafab.ai/" data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1">Terafab</a> in Austin, Texas, a joint venture between Musk's companies like SpaceX, Tesla and xAI to manufacture the chips necessary to power various AI projects. Musk <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/science/elon-musk-announces-terafab-project-he-claims-will-be-the-largest-chip-manufacturing-facility-ever-171718545.html" data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1">announced Terafab</a> in March 2026 with the plan of eventually creating a terawatt of computing power each year.</p><p>While Tesla and SpaceX have experience manufacturing in the US, chip fabrication plants like the ones Intel runs are expensive and time-consuming to build. Offloading the task of actually building the Terafab from Musk's companies to Intel makes sense. "Our ability to design, fabricate, and package ultra-high-performance chips at scale will help accelerate Terafab’s aim to produce 1 TW/year of compute to power future advances in AI and robotics," Intel said in its announcement.</p><div><blockquote class="twitter-tweet" style="width:640px;height:893px;"><p lang="en" dir="ltr">Intel is proud to join the Terafab project with <a href="https://twitter.com/SpaceX?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw">@SpaceX</a>, <a href="https://twitter.com/xai?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw">@xAI</a>, and <a href="https://twitter.com/Tesla?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw">@Tesla</a> to help refactor silicon fab technology.<br><br>Our ability to design, fabricate, and package ultra-high-performance chips at scale will help accelerate Terafab’s aim to produce 1 TW/year of compute to power… <a href="https://t.co/2vUmXn0YhH">pic.twitter.com/2vUmXn0YhH</a></p>— Intel (@intel) <a href="https://twitter.com/intel/status/2041501301318766866?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw">April 7, 2026</a></blockquote>


</div><p>Musk's plan to produce chips is part of a larger refocusing of his various companies around AI. For example, Tesla has gone from an electric car company to <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/transportation/evs/tesla-is-killing-off-its-model-s-and-x-cars-to-make-robots-010621101.html" data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1">a robotics company</a>, and SpaceX is now <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/science/space/blue-origin-also-wants-to-put-ai-data-centers-in-space-115614142.html" data-i13n="cpos:5;pos:1">one of several aerospace companies</a> hoping to <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/science/space/spacex-wants-to-launch-a-constellation-of-a-million-satellites-to-power-ai-needs-175607771.html" data-i13n="cpos:6;pos:1">launch AI data centers into space</a>. Making those intentions even more clear, SpaceX also <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/ai/elon-musks-spacex-has-acquired-his-ai-company-xai-221617040.html" data-i13n="cpos:7;pos:1">acquired Musk's AI company xAI</a> in February 2026 and now <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/big-tech/spacex-has-reportedly-filed-for-the-biggest-ipo-in-history-154547537.html" data-i13n="cpos:8;pos:1">reportedly plans to go public</a>.</p><p>Intel is in a slightly better position now than it was a year ago thanks to the launch of its new <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/computing/ces-2026-intel-hopes-its-core-ultra-series-3-chips-are-the-start-of-a-comeback-000155611.html" data-i13n="cpos:9;pos:1">Intel Core Ultra Series 3 chips</a> and <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/big-tech/the-us-government-is-taking-an-89-billion-stake-in-intel-205047795.html" data-i13n="cpos:10;pos:1">direct investment from the US government</a> in August 2025, but the company has <a target="_blank" class="no-affiliate-link link" href="https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/22/intel-intc-earnings-report-q4-2025-.html" data-i13n="elm:context_link;elmt:doNotAffiliate;cpos:11;pos:1">plenty of its own issues</a> to iron out. It’s also still working to get two separate chip fabs in Arizona operating at full capacity, a project it <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/intel-7nm-2023-meteor-lake-213933055.html" data-i13n="cpos:12;pos:1">originally announced in 2021</a>.</p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/ai/intel-gets-on-board-with-musks-terafab-project-182200144.html?src=rss
