<p>Meta is killing end-to-end encryption in Instagram DMs. The feature will "no longer be supported after May 8, 2026," the company wrote in <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://help.instagram.com/491565145294150/?helpref=uf_share" data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1">an update</a> on its support page. Unlike WhatsApp, Meta never made encryption available to all Instagram users and it was never a default setting. Instead, users in "some areas" had the ability <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://help.instagram.com/1165835007222763/?helpref=related_articles" data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1">to opt-in</a> to encryption on a per-chat basis.</p><p>In a statement, a Meta spokesperson said the feature was being retired due to low adoption. "Very few people were opting in to end-to-end encrypted messaging in DMs, so we're removing this option from Instagram in the coming months," the spokesperson said. "Anyone who wants to keep messaging with end-to-end encryption can easily do that on WhatsApp.”</p><p>Interestingly, Meta's statement doesn't mention the status of encryption on Messenger. The company began turning on end-to-end encryption as a default setting <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/facebook-messenger-update-sets-default-end-to-end-encryption-for-chats-and-calls-032841879.html" data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1">in 2023</a> after years of work on the feature. It started encrypting WhatsApp chats in 2016. In 2019, Mark Zuckerberg outlined a "<a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/2019-03-06-mark-zuckerberg-outlines-facebook-privacy-rethink.html" data-i13n="cpos:5;pos:1">privacy-focused</a>" revamp of the company's apps, <a target="_blank" class="link rapid-with-clickid" href="https://shopping.yahoo.com/rdlw?merchantId=58a137b8-4748-4ad2-b1db-003c79a332f5&amp;siteId=us-engadget&amp;pageId=1p-autolink&amp;contentUuid=07a79c9b-4669-4762-87f8-e0e6e7328d1a&amp;featureId=text-link&amp;merchantName=Facebook&amp;linkText=saying&amp;custData=eyJzb3VyY2VOYW1lIjoiV2ViLURlc2t0b3AtVmVyaXpvbiIsImxhbmRpbmdVcmwiOiJodHRwczovL3d3dy5mYWNlYm9vay5jb20vbm90ZXMvMjQyMDYwMDI1ODIzNDE3Mi8iLCJjb250ZW50VXVpZCI6IjA3YTc5YzliLTQ2NjktNDc2Mi04N2Y4LWUwZTZlNzMyOGQxYSIsIm9yaWdpbmFsVXJsIjoiaHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuZmFjZWJvb2suY29tL25vdGVzLzI0MjA2MDAyNTgyMzQxNzIvIn0&amp;signature=AQAAAeCoPHYR9C5njkjUTiLGHhxK6FxkeWD8uYC8hOa4DTnK&amp;gcReferrer=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.facebook.com%2Fnotes%2F2420600258234172%2F" data-i13n="elm:affiliate_link;sellerN:Facebook;elmt:;cpos:6;pos:1" data-original-link="https://www.facebook.com/notes/2420600258234172/">saying</a> at the time that "implementing end-to-end encryption for all private communications is the right thing to do." In 2021, the company's head of safety said that Meta was delaying its encryption work <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://archive.is/20211121133315/https://www.telegraph.co.uk/business/2021/11/20/people-shouldnt-have-choose-privacy-safety-says-facebook-safety/" data-i13n="cpos:7;pos:1">until 2023</a> in order to create stronger safety features.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p><p>Meta’s use of encryption has been repeatedly criticized by law enforcement and some child safety organizations that say the feature makes it harder to catch predators who target children on social media. Recently, the topic has been raised numerous times during a trial <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/social-media/mark-zuckerberg-downplays-metas-own-research-in-new-mexico-child-safety-trial-222924340.html" data-i13n="cpos:8;pos:1">in New Mexico</a> over child safety. Internal documents that have surfaced as part of the trial show Meta executives and researchers debating the trade-offs between safety and privacy as it relates to encryption.&nbsp;</p><p>In testimony that was broadcast during the trial, Zuckerberg said that safety issues were "a large part of the reason why it took so long" to bring encryption to Messenger. "There's been debate about this, but I think the majority of folks, from people who use our products to people who are involved in security overall, believe that strong encryption is positive," he said.</p><p><br></p><p></p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/social-media/meta-is-killing-end-to-end-encryption-in-instagram-dms-195207421.html?src=rss
