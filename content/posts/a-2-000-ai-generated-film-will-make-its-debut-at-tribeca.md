<p class="has-text-align-none">Next month's Tribeca Festival will include the premiere of an AI-generated film: <em>Dreams of Violets</em>. The 75-minute film is a fictional dramatization of the <a href="https://www.hrw.org/news/2026/01/16/iran-growing-evidence-of-countrywide-massacres">Iranian government's mass killing</a> of protestors in January, with the people and images fully created by AI, as <a href="https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/movies/movie-news/ai-film-dreams-of-violet-tribeca-premiere-1236606092/">reported earlier by <em>The Hollywood Reporter</em></a><em>.</em></p>
<p class="has-text-align-none"><em>Dreams of Violets</em> cost $2,000 to make and is "based on journalistic reports, photographs, and eyewitness accounts," <a href="https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260527634103/en/Dreams-of-Violets-to-World-Premiere-at-Tribeca-Festival-as-the-First-Feature-Length-Live-Action-Film-Completely-Generated-by-AI-Accepted-to-a-Major-Film-Festival">according to a press release</a>. It was created by Ash and Pooya Koosha, two brothers who left Iran in 2009. Pooya cofounded Fountain 0, the company behind the film, while Ash serves as CEO.</p>
