<h2 id="jump-link-what-to-expect">What to expect</h2>
<p>NVIDIA&#39;s game plan for CES is suitably vague so far, including  &quot;cutting-edge AI, robotics, simulation, gaming and content creation at the NVIDIA Showcase.&quot; It also notes there will be more than 20 demos. Although we&#39;re unsure if all of these will be shown during the keynote, we can at least expect to see them throughout the week of CES.</p>
<p>Given <a data-i13n="cpos:5;pos:1" href="https://finance.yahoo.com/news/nvidia-worth-much-german-economy-133045033.html">NVIDIA&#39;s sky-high valuation</a> and the fact that the health of the US and global economy seems <a data-i13n="cpos:6;pos:1" href="https://finance.yahoo.com/news/spending-ai-data-centers-massive-150110727.html">increasingly linked to infrastructure spending on AI data centers</a> – largely powered by chips from NVIDIA and its competitors — expect Huang&#39;s remarks to be as closely followed by Wall Street investors as technology acolytes, if not more so. Will we get any insight on a successor to the company&#39;s Blackwell chip? A more detailed look at how NVIDIA&#39;s partners are applying AI to real-world robotics?
