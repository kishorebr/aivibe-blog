<p class="has-drop-cap has-text-align-none">Colin Angle, the maker of the Roomba and the man who helped put <a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/roomba-irobot-bankruptcy-warning-rise-fall-2025-11">50 million household robots</a> into people's homes, is back with a new robot. But this one is designed as a companion, not a cleaner.</p>
<p class="has-text-align-none">The first robot from Angle's new company, <a href="https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/roomba-pioneer-colin-angle-unveils-new-venture-familiar-machines--magic-introducing-a-new-platform-for-consumer-physical-ai-302761495.html">Familiar Machines &amp; Magic</a>, is a dog-sized robotic pet that resembles a cross between a bear, a barn owl, and a golden retriever. It has an expressive face, with movable eyebrows, ears, and eyes, and the company calls it a "Familiar," a name meant to evoke folklore around the idea of a supernatural companion.
