<p>XChat, the standalone app for accessing X's messaging feature, is available to download now <a target="_blank" class="no-affiliate-link link" href="https://apps.apple.com/us/app/xchat/id6760873038" data-i13n="elm:context_link;elmt:doNotAffiliate;cpos:1;pos:1">for iOS</a>. X first suggested it would be <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/social-media/x-may-soon-replace-dms-with-its-upcoming-chat-platform-171006540.html" data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1">stripping direct messaging from X in 2025</a>, but at least for now, XChat is available in the original X app, the web and this new app.</p><p><a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://x.com/chat/status/2047747085362139463?s=20" data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1">Based on its launch video</a>, the new XChat app offers many of the elements of modern messaging X had <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/social-media/x-is-finally-rolling-out-chat-its-dm-replacement-with-encryption-and-video-calling-233032571.html" data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1">already introduced to its chats feature</a>, like the ability to delete and edit messages, block screenshots and send disappearing messages. The new XChat app also supports video and audio calls, and X claims that all messages sent with XChat are end-to-end encrypted.</p><p>XChat will also be expected to be the home of any groups that formed around X's Communities feature. The social platform <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/social-media/x-is-shutting-down-its-communities-feature-182843958.html" data-i13n="cpos:5;pos:1">recently announced</a> that it was retiring Communities at the end of May, and suggested that XChat's support for larger group chats could be a worthwhile alternative. XChat's group chats can currently have 350 participants, but X plans to expand that number in the future.</p><div><blockquote class="twitter-tweet" style="width:640px;height:771px;"><p lang="en" dir="ltr">The everything app, which requires 3 apps to use the core product. <a href="https://t.co/1aJF4n2par">pic.twitter.com/1aJF4n2par</a></p>— camol (@camolNFT) <a href="https://twitter.com/camolNFT/status/2047325358865662216?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw">April 23, 2026</a></blockquote>


</div><p>Elon Musk's original pitch after he rebranded Twitter as X, was to turn the platform into an <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://x.com/elonmusk/status/1577428272056389633?s=20" data-i13n="cpos:6;pos:1">"everything app,"</a> where things like an algorithmic feed, messaging, <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/xs-job-search-tool-is-now-live-on-the-web-010200007.html" data-i13n="cpos:7;pos:1">job boards</a> and even <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/social-media/xs-payments-app-will-be-available-later-this-year-181046773.html" data-i13n="cpos:8;pos:1">payments</a> could exist side-by-side. A standalone messaging app seems like the exact opposite of that, but it might also reflect where X finds itself in 2026. The company is now <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/social-media/xai-elon-musks-ai-company-just-purchased-x-elon-musks-social-media-company-221503759.html" data-i13n="cpos:9;pos:1">a subsidiary of xAI</a>, and xAI itself <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/ai/elon-musks-spacex-has-acquired-his-ai-company-xai-221617040.html" data-i13n="cpos:10;pos:1">is part of SpaceX</a>. Musk's push into AI appears to be the going concern, and cloning something like WeChat might just be less important.</p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/apps/xchat-the-standalone-app-for-messaging-on-x-is-available-on-ios-now-214826886.html?src=rss
