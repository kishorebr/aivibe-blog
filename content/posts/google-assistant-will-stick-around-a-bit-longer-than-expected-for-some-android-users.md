<p>Google <a target="_blank" class="linkDecorator_linkStyle__yM-Nh link" href="https://www.engadget.com/ai/google-is-removing-assistant-from-most-phones-this-year-182700104.html" data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1">wanted to remove Assistant</a> from most Android phones by the end of 2025 and replace it with Gemini. But now the company has <a target="_blank" class="linkDecorator_linkStyle__yM-Nh link" href="https://support.google.com/gemini/thread/396052272/here%E2%80%99s-an-update-on-our-work-to-upgrade-mobile-assistant-devices-to-gemini" data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1">announced</a> that it needs a bit more time to make its AI assistant the new default digital helper for most of its users. Google said that it&#39;s adjusting its previously announced timeline to &quot;make sure [it delivers] a seamless transition&quot; and that updates to convert Assistant to Gemini on Android devices will continue into the next year. The company also said that it&#39;s sharing more details in the &quot;coming months,&quot; so it&#39;s possible that the transition will go past early 2026.</p><p>Assistant&#39;s retirement was pretty much expected the moment Google launched Gemini and started giving it Assistant&#39;s capabilities, such as the ability to control smart devices connected to your phone. It launched the Pixel 9 Series with Gemini as the <a target="_blank" class="linkDecorator_linkStyle__yM-Nh link" href="https://www.engadget.com/ai/gemini-googles-ai-powered-chatbot-is-the-default-assistant-on-pixel-9-phones-170027220.html" data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1">default assistant</a> back in 2024. The company has also been putting Gemini in all of its products and previously said that it plans to upgrade all &quot;tablets, cars and devices that connect to your phone, such as headphones and watches&quot; with the AI-powered chatbot. Devices do have to meet a few minimum requirements to get the upgrade, however, and must be running Android 10 and come with 2GB of RAM at the very least.</p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/ai/google-assistant-will-stick-around-a-bit-longer-than-expected-for-some-android-users-130000178.html?src=rss
