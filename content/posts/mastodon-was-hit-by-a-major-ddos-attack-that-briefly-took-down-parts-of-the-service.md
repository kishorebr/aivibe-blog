<p>Mastodon seems to be recovering after a Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attack that took down its primary mastodon.social instance. As <em>TechCrunch </em><a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/20/mastodon-says-its-flagship-server-was-hit-by-a-ddos-attack/" data-i13n="slk:notes;cpos:1;pos:1">notes</a>, the platform began <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://status.mastodon.social/cmo730jrm05ailojla22drifc" data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1">reporting issues</a> early Monday morning as much of the Mastodon-operated server became inaccessible.&nbsp;</p><p>It&#39;s not clear who might be behind the attack, but Mastodon&#39;s head of communications Andy Piper <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://macaw.social/@andypiper/116436859213678675" data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1">described it</a> as a &quot;major&quot; incident. A couple hours later, Mastodon shared on a status page that it had implemented countermeasures and that users should be able to access mastodon.social once again. Piper said that &quot;some ongoing instability is a possibility&quot; as the site recovered. It&#39;s unclear if any other instances of the service were also targeted; mastodon.social is run directly by the nonprofit and is the largest server on the federated platform.&nbsp;</p><p>Mastodon is the second decentralized platform to be targeted with a DDoS in recent days. Last week, Bluesky also dealt with a significant DDoS incident that took parts of the service offline for several hours. The company posted what it said was its <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://x.com/bluesky/status/2046274845617365039" data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1">final update</a> Monday morning, saying that its service had &quot;remained stable&quot; and that there was &quot;no evidence of unauthorized access to private user data.&quot; A few hours later, however, it seemed Bluesky was once again experiencing some issues, though the cause was unclear. Its official <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://status.bsky.app/" data-i13n="cpos:5;pos:1">status page </a>was down, and a post from its <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://bsky.app/profile/status.bsky.app/post/3mjx6iwke4s2a" data-i13n="cpos:6;pos:1">server status</a> account indicated that there were &quot;elevated errors and timeouts on some Bluesky-hosted services.&quot; Bluesky said it was investigating.</p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/social-media/mastodon-was-hit-by-a-major-ddos-attack-that-briefly-took-down-parts-of-the-service-204823221.html?src=rss
