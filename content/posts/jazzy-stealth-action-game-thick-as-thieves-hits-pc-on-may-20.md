<p><em>Thick as Thieves</em>, the magical heist game from a crew of stealth legends at OtherSide Entertainment, is <a data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1" href="https://store.steampowered.com/app/3341000/Thick_As_Thieves/">coming to Steam</a> on May 20. OtherSide and publisher Megabit Entertainment announced the release date in a new gameplay trailer during today&#39;s <a data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1" href="https://www.engadget.com/gaming/how-to-watch-the-triple-i-initiative-showcase-on-april-9-170353957.html">Triple-i Initiative showcase</a> (which was <a data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1" href="https://www.engadget.com/gaming/indie-game-neverway-will-launch-this-october-just-in-time-for-spooky-season-170000669.html">packed</a> <a data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1" href="https://www.engadget.com/gaming/another-dont-starve-game-is-on-the-way-184400774.html">with</a> <a data-i13n="cpos:5;pos:1" href="https://www.engadget.com/gaming/1000xresist-devs-reveal-their-wild-looking-second-game-about-convincing-an-ai-its-not-human-170018986.html">cool</a> <a data-i13n="cpos:6;pos:1" href="https://www.engadget.com/gaming/co-op-pirate-survival-game-windrose-hits-pc-in-early-access-on-april-14-175842163.html">indie</a> <a data-i13n="cpos:7;pos:1" href="https://www.engadget.com/gaming/mountain-climbing-sim-cairn-is-getting-free-dlc-this-summer-182908992.html">games</a>, as always).</p>
<p>OtherSide was founded by Thief and <em>System Shock 2</em> veteran Paul Neurath in 2013, and Deus Ex creator Warren Spector joined in 2016. These are two of the developers who shaped the stealth genre as we know it, so <em>Thick as Thieves</em> has a solid foundation. The new trailer showcases the game&#39;s fictional Scottish city of Kilcairn, where magic and technology clash against a backdrop of 1910s music and architecture. At launch, <em>Thick as Thieves</em> will feature two thieves, two maps, 16 contracts, six unique pieces of gear and multiple difficulty settings, which alter the layout of each level.</p>

<p><em>Thick as Thieves</em> has evolved since its <a data-i13n="cpos:8;pos:1" href="https://www.engadget.com/gaming/pc/thick-as-thieves-is-a-multiplayer-stealth-action-game-from-legends-warren-spector-and-paul-neurath-004352183.html">introduction</a> at The Game Awards in December 2024. It was originally pitched as a PvPvE game, but it&#39;s landing as a solo or two-player experience, with no player-on-player brawls. OtherSide announced the pivot on Steam on <a data-i13n="cpos:9;pos:1" href="https://store.steampowered.com/news/app/3341000/view/519742851965256606?l=english">April 2, 2026</a>:</p>
<p>&quot;As development progressed and the world of Kilcairn has come to life, we found that we were having more fun with solo and co-op play. So, we’ve taken a decision we feel is right for the game: <em>Thick as Thieves</em> is now focused on two-player co-op and single player. This sharpened focus has allowed us to double down on what makes <em>Thick as Thieves</em> truly special — dynamic stealth gameplay.&quot;</p>
<p>A handful of playtesters and early fans expressed their disappointment at the removal of PvP. A developer <a data-i13n="cpos:10;pos:1" href="https://steamcommunity.com/app/3341000/eventcomments/802343728582881705?snr=1_5_9_#c802343728582883877">responded</a> to one question with, &quot;PvPvE may come further down the line.&quot;</p>
<p>There&#39;s also no word on the console versions of the game. <em>Thick as Thieves</em> was announced for PC, PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X/S, but today&#39;s news suggests it&#39;s coming to Steam first (if not only). Details are a bit up in the air at the moment, but hey, at least it&#39;s on-brand for the developers of a stealth game to be sneaky.</p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/gaming/pc/jazzy-stealth-action-game-thick-as-thieves-hits-pc-on-may-20-193320746.html?src=rss
