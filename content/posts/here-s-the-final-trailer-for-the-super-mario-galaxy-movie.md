<p><em>The Super Mario Galaxy Movie</em> <a data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1" href="https://www.engadget.com/entertainment/tv-movies/the-new-trailer-for-the-super-mario-galaxy-movie-shows-yoshi-absolutely-devouring-a-magikoopa-191807037.html"><ins>is nearly upon us</ins></a>, as the hotly-anticipated sequel arrives in theaters on April 1. Nintendo recently dropped the <a data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LX9kXRRJlPw"><ins>final trailer for the film</ins></a>, which is filled with quick visual gags and nods to the source material.</p>
<div id="4f9aacab188642bbb4cf683f1e281ebc"><iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/LX9kXRRJlPw?si=G8AoWPpRDF71pwWy" title="YouTube video player" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></div>
<p>There aren&#39;t too many actual reveals in this footage, as it covers a lot of the same ground as previous trailers. However, it does show that fan favorite Lumalee is returning as a prison guard of some sort, reversing the storyline from the original film in which the cheerfully nihilistic creature was trapped in a cage.</p>
<span id="end-legacy-contents"></span><p>Nintendo <a data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fbaZAQMwEbc&amp;t=346s"><ins>also released a larger presentation</ins></a> that featured the aforementioned trailer, but also included interviews with actors and franchise creator Shigeru Miyamoto. We did get some news in this video.</p>
<p>It was revealed that the long-tongued dinosaur Yoshi will be voiced by Donald Glover. So it&#39;s likely the dino will be saying a lot more than &quot;Yoshi&quot; over and over. Actor Luis Guzman will also be playing Wart, the primary antagonist from <em>Super Mario Bros. 2</em>. Issa Rae will be on hand to voice Honey Queen, the gigantic bee character from the Super Mario Galaxy games.</p>
<div id="a9ef36b594c44a78a457a04712610941"><iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/fbaZAQMwEbc?si=Maq8feZMKOKFUIhA" title="YouTube video player" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></div>
<p>It was even confirmed by lead actors Chris Pratt and Charlie Day that Luigi would be on hand for the entire adventure this time, and not confined to a cage-based subplot. I didn&#39;t realize Luigi&#39;s role in the first film was <a data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1" href="https://screenrant.com/super-mario-bros-movie-luigi-missing-reason/"><ins>enough of a controversy</ins></a> to warrant this kind of mention, but here we are.</p>
<p>Illumination CEO Chris Meledandri also appeared in the video, assuring viewers that there are still &quot;some big surprises&quot; waiting in the actual film. To that end, there&#39;s been a <a data-i13n="cpos:5;pos:1" href="https://www.ign.com/articles/nintendo-fans-believe-the-super-mario-galaxy-movie-has-a-star-fox-cameo-after-trailer-teases-mysterious-figure"><ins>rumor floating around</ins></a> that Fox McCloud from the Starfox franchise would be showing up. Is this the start of a Nintendo cinematic universe that will culminate in 10 years with a Super Smash Bros. movie?
