<p class="has-text-align-none">The past few days have been a wild ride for xAI, which is racking up staff and cofounder departure announcements left and right. On Tuesday and Wednesday, cofounder Yuhuai (Tony) Wu <a href="https://x.com/Yuhu_ai_/status/2021113745024614671?s=20">announced</a> his departure and that it was "time for [his] next chapter," with cofounder Jimmy Ba following with a <a href="https://x.com/jimmybajimmyba/status/2021374875793801447?s=20">similar post</a> later that day, writing that it was "time to recalibrate [his] gradient on the big picture." The departures mean that xAI is now left with <a href="https://www.reuters.com/business/two-co-founders-elon-musks-xai-resign-joining-exodus-2026-02-11/#:~:text=By%20Reuters,next%20plans%2C%20but%20thanked%20Musk.">only half</a> of its original 12 cofounders on staff. <a href="https://x.com/Chace_AGI/status/2021452881875141060?s=20">A</a> <a href="https://x.com/hangg70/status/2021412690321924543?s=20">number</a> <a href="https://x.com/ayushjaiswal/status/2019857451823296876?s=20">of</a> <a href="https://x.com/simon_zhai/status/2020980340895121869?s=20">staffers</a> <a href="https://x.com/textangel/status/2021718007433900230?s=46&amp;t=fRkDIqgNCkTkvg8ZBiLA9A">also</a> took to X to announce they were leaving xAI, with some announcing that they were starting their own AI companies. </p>
