<p>A film adaptation of <em>Metal Gear Solid</em> is in the works again, this time from filmmakers Zach Lipovsky and Adam B. Stein, the directors of <em>Final Destination: Bloodlines</em>, <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/movies/movie-news/metal-gear-solid-movie-works-1236560289/" data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1">according to <em>The Hollywood Reporter</em></a><em>.</em> The duo are reviving the project at Columbia Pictures as part of a new first-look deal with Sony, the latest attempt in what&#39;s been multiple decades of work to turn the blockbuster stealth game into a blockbuster film.</p><p>&quot;Metal Gear Solid was nothing short of a groundbreaking cinematic masterpiece that forever revolutionized video games,&quot; Lipovsky and Stein said in a statement. &quot;We are thrilled and honored to bring Hideo Kojima&#39;s iconic characters and unforgettable world to life.&quot;</p><p>Lipovsky and Stein&#39;s horror bona fides helped make <em>Bloodlines</em> <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.indiewire.com/news/box-office/final-destination-bloodlines-box-office-analysis-1235124573/" data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1">a critical and commercial hit</a> when it came out in 2025, and the directors have a variety of other IP-focused genre films in the works, including <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/movies/movie-news/gremlins-3-final-destination-screenwriters-1236371957/" data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1">a sequel to <em>Gremlins</em></a> for Warner Bros. and <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/movies/movie-news/venom-animated-movie-in-the-works-1236510453/" data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1">an animated Venom movie</a> for Sony. It remains to be seen how exactly the duo will translate <em>Metal Gear Solid</em>&#39;s unique quirks to film, though.</p><p><em>Metal Gear Solid </em>is heavily indebted to director Hideo Kojima&#39;s own taste in action and spy cinema, while also being in conversation with video games themselves in a way that wouldn&#39;t naturally translate to film. And even if you removed those metatextual rough edges, can it really be <em>Metal Gear Solid</em> without Kojima&#39;s <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V9LylmwNdM0" data-i13n="cpos:5;pos:1">equal parts charming and awkward writing</a>?&nbsp;</p><p>Attempts to create a film version of the game date back to 2006, when Kojima <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/2006-05-15-metal-gear-solid-movie-confirmed-definitively.html" data-i13n="cpos:6;pos:1">first shared</a> that an adaptation was in the works. Columbia Pictures <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2012/aug/30/metal-gear-solid-movie-announced" data-i13n="cpos:7;pos:1">announced a new version of the film</a> in 2012, with Avi Arad, former head of Marvel Studios, producing. In 2014, Jordan Vogt-Roberts, the director of <em>Kong: Skull Island</em>, was <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://deadline.com/2014/06/sony-in-talks-with-jordan-vogt-roberts-to-helm-metal-gear-solid-739744/" data-i13n="cpos:8;pos:1">attached to direct</a> that adaptation. And six years after that, Oscar Isaac <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/oscar-issac-solid-snake-metal-gear-solid-movie-183642573.html" data-i13n="cpos:9;pos:1">was reportedly cast as Solid Snake</a>. Arad and his son Ari Arad are still producing this latest take on the game, but with Lipovsky and Stein in charge, that older version of <em>Metal Gear Solid</em> is likely dead. Still, hope springs eternal that we’ll get to see a man hide in a cardboard box on the big screen someday.</p><p></p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/entertainment/tv-movies/the-metal-gear-solid-movie-is-back-on-with-final-destination-bloodlines-directors-in-charge-214914374.html?src=rss
