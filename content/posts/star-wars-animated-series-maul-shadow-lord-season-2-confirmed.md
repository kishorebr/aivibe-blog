<p>Ahead of its <a data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1" href="https://www.engadget.com/entertainment/streaming/darth-mauls-standalone-series-premieres-on-disney-on-april-6-224036720.html">premiere</a>, Dave Filoni has revealed that the Star Wars animated series <em>Maul: Shadow Lord</em> will return for a second season. The Lucasfilm co-president revealed that season 2 is already in the works, telling <a data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1" href="https://www.esquire.com/entertainment/tv/a70898555/darth-maul-shadow-lord-dave-filoni-interview/"><em>Esquire</em></a> that &quot;at the end of the day, people like that character.&quot;&nbsp;</p>
<p>Filoni didn&#39;t reveal any other details about the plot or release date for season 2. However, the news isn&#39;t a great surprise given Lucasfilm&#39;s past history with its animated series — <em>The Clone Wars</em> ran seven seasons, <em>Star Wars Rebels</em> four seasons, <em>Star Wars Resistance</em> two seasons and <em>Star Wars: The Bad Batch</em> three seasons.&nbsp;</p>
<span id="end-legacy-contents"></span><p><em>Maul: Shadow Lord</em> explores the Zebrak Sith Lord&#39;s story about a year after the time of the Clone Wars. Season 1&#39;s 10 seasons will stream twice a week on Disney+ starting on April 6 and run through May 6. It covers Maul&#39;s plot to rebuild his criminal syndicate &quot;on a planet untouched by the Empire,&quot; <a data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1" href="https://www.starwars.com/news/star-wars-maul-shadow-lord-first-trailer-poster-art">according to Lucasfilm</a>. &quot;There, he crosses paths with a disillusioned young Jedi Padawan who may just be the apprentice he is seeking to aid him in his relentless pursuit for revenge.&quot;</p>
