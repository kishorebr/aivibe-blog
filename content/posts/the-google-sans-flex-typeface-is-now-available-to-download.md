<p>Typography nerds and Android fans, rejoice: You can now download an official version of “the next generation of Google’s brand typeface.” The company has released the Google Sans Flex font to the public for free.</p><p>The variable sans-serif font is part of <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.engadget.com/google-rollout-new-look-docs-workspace-apps-124542742.html" data-i13n="cpos:1;pos:1">Google&#39;s Material 3 design language</a>, which arrived in 2023. <em>9to5Google</em> <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://9to5google.com/2025/11/18/google-sans-flex-font-available/" data-i13n="cpos:2;pos:1">notes</a> that it&#39;s since been integrated into many of the company&#39;s products, including in some corners of Pixel software.</p><p>A 2024 Google Design <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://design.google/library/variable-fonts-type" data-i13n="cpos:3;pos:1">blog post</a> about variable typography highlights the font&#39;s flexibility, as seen in the image above. Casey Henry, a designer with the company, wrote that Google Sans Flex &quot;allows the font&#39;s letterforms to shape-shift at different scales.&quot; <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://opensource.googleblog.com/2016/09/introducing-opentype-font-variations.html" data-i13n="cpos:4;pos:1">OpenType Font Variations</a> is the standard Google uses for variable fonts.</p><p>Meanwhile, a Reddit thread about the download dove deeper into typography nerdery. “Interesting behaviour when you condense the width,” u/hbpencil102 <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://www.reddit.com/r/typography/comments/1ozdu65/finally_the_google_font_google_sans_is_opensource/" data-i13n="cpos:5;pos:1">wrote</a>. “Instead of circles becoming ovals, they become more rectangular with rounded tops and bottoms, reminding me of DIN 1451.” Amen to that.</p><p>You can download Google Sans Flex from <a target="_blank" class="link" href="https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Google+Sans+Flex/about" data-i13n="cpos:6;pos:1">Google Fonts</a>.</p>This article originally appeared on Engadget at https://www.engadget.com/big-tech/the-google-sans-flex-typeface-is-now-available-to-download-214535934.html?src=rss
