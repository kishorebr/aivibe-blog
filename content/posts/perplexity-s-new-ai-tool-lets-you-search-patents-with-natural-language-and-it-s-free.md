    Perplexity Patents is currently in beta and free for all users. Here's how to try it.
      Written by

If someone wanted to find patents in a certain area, they'd have to use several tools, like Google Patents, the United States Patent and Trademark Office, or a global patent database. Then, they'd have to search using specific keywords or codes -- or even rely on a patent attorney for supreme accuracy. Also: Why AI coding tools like Cursor and Replit are doomed - and what comes nextPerplexity Patents, the AI company's latest tool, aims to not only democratize but also speed up that process.
